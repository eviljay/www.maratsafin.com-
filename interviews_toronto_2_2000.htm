After the match in the semifinal (M. Safin -W.Ferreira)
at Tennis Masters Series in Toronto Satarday,
5th of August 2000
Safin d. Ferreira 6-2, 5-7, 6-4




TOUR OFFICIAL: Okay. Marat is with us. Before we start, this is Marat's third career Tennis Masters Series final, the second of the year and the last one he lost to Gustavo Kuerten and he's also trying to win his third ATP title. First question in the front.
_

REPORTER: Marat, from what the television showed, you looked as surprised as Wayne did at what was happening at the end of the match when he was penalized. Can you just talk about what happened from your point of view?

M. SAFIN: Simple and also a very difficult thing. Because if we start from the beginning, it starts with the ball abuse from him. He throw the ball out of the court. I mean, it's a little bit, I think sometimes it's not right because you have 12,000 people watching us. I hope they enjoy our game. And why, because he throw the ball out of the court?
I mean, he didn't kill anybody, he didn't shout, he didn't say any bad words. I mean, he was nervous. It's a semifinal and the people, I think, they understood perfectly this. I think the result is not something strange. Okay, it's a warning, right. And after, in the last game, 15-love, it was a winner, on his forehand. For me, the ball was in. So the linesman, he said, "Out." So I just went like this (indicating). Because I saw clearly that it was in. Because I'm not this kind of people if the ball is out I start to discuss. For me it was in, so that's why I just put my hands on the head. Also, Rudi, he overrule it. I think, if I am talking from his part, from Wayne, why he has to overrule it, when the score is 6-5 -- it's 6-5 or 5-4? 5-4, 15-love, and for 15-all. Why overrule, if you never overrule during all the match? Also a strange situation.
I think that it's also a little bit absurd when he stop. I mean, when he stopped. It's okay. It's 12,000 people, he stopped, he didn't play I don't know for one minute, it doesn't matter. It make him -- he took too much time between the points. It's a little bit strange. But the rules are the rules. I can understand Rudi, and I can understand Wayne. But, I mean, it's difficult to say, "No, Wayne is right" or "Rudi is right." I mean, Rudi, he has his rules and he has to do it. Also, it's difficult to decide at an important moment if the ball was in or the ball was out. You cannot overrule it in the end of the match if you didn't overrule it during all the match. So, it's a little bit strange situation.

REPORTER: Marat, after all that was said and done, you served for the match and he just let's the ball go and doesn't even make an attempt. Did that bother you, or were you surprised that he didn't even attempt?

M. SAFIN: No, actually, I can understand him perfectly. When the guy, they overruled his point, for him it's "Guys, I'm trying to win this match and you make me this mistake." Or whatever. It's a little bit strange. So he said, "Okay. If you do like this, I will do like that." And the people they understood perfectly, no? So it's a little bit -- it's much easier for me, but actually -- I'm not happy because he stopped the ball. I think he didn't have so many chances to beat me from 40-love. I don't think that -- if he'd returned once, okay. But I have a little more of advantage.

REPORTER: Marat, after having a long, tough match, beating Pete Sampras last night, how much of a sense was there that you -- maybe a let down, trying to pump yourself back up? Because you started off looking like you were going to finish Ferreira off early, but then you let him back into the match. Was there a bit of a let down for you?

M. SAFIN: No, I think I have enough experience, I don't have to -- just because I beat Pete Sampras, it's not the end of the world, unfortunately, it's not. I just forgot it. I knew that I had to play against Wayne, it's another difficult match. It's a good opportunity to be in the final. So you don't think about Pete, or I beat Pete. I know he's the best player in the world, but, I mean, that's over. I beat him yesterday. Today is another day. You have to work also, you have to start from the beginning, from 0-0, and trying to beat Wayne. It's a little bit different what happened with me because I had the match almost done, 6-2, 4-3, and serve or something like. But it was a question of -- I just stopped, I didn't move enough to play. Because I thought that, okay, I will put the ball in and he will miss it. But he almost beat me. It was a question not because of Pete Sampras, because I think I just stopped. I get a little maybe afraid at some points, but that was the point.

REPORTER: You played Andre in Paris, and you played Gustavo for the last Masters Series. Now you play Harel Levy. How do you feel about that?

M. SAFIN: The same like I felt for all the finals. Because if the person is in the final, that means that he's playing great tennis. I mean, he passed through the qualis. He beat so many guys. He beat something like this.

TOUR OFFICIAL: Seven.

M. SAFIN: Seven, eight. But I think it means that he's playing his best tennis. He can beat me. If he beat Novak, he beat Grosjean, he beat Golmard, why he cannot beat me? I'm also thinking about like this about him. If I beat Sampras, I beat Ferreira, I beat Pavel, I beat Bjorkman and Escude, why not Levy? It is a little bit same situation, but I know for you guys maybe Levy doesn't sound a lot, but for me it's because I have to be in the court tomorrow, I have to try to beat him. It's a little bit different. If he's in the final, he's in good shape.

REPORTER: This is a bit of a crazy question, about your coaches. Can I make some suggestion to you about coaches and you say what you think, just for fun?

M. SAFIN: What?

REPORTER: I'll mention some coaches and -- you're looking for a coach still, aren't you?

M. SAFIN: Yes.

REPORTER: So it's better if you have a coach who speaks your language, so what about Cherkasov?

M. SAFIN: Cherkasov? I talked about him, but the point is I don't want to lose him like a friend. It's more important to have Cherky like a friend, not like a coach. Because if there is -- something happen, something, one small misunderstanding, I will lose him like friend. And I don't want this. I prefer to find another coach, another guy that I don't know so well like Cherky, because he is too nice for me, a nice guy, and I don't want to lose him.

REPORTER: If you want someone who speaks the language, what about a Spanish player like Javier Sanchez, or Sergio Casal, something like that?

M. SAFIN: He has enough millions in the bank account to work with me.

REPORTER: Do you have anybody in mind at all?

M. SAFIN: Unfortunately, no. But I have time and I'm not doing so bad without coaches. I mean, I would have to think about this, but I will try to think about the coaching after U.S. Open, after the Super 9's, after the Grand Slams, and after I have enough time to think.

TOUR OFFICIAL: Anything else for Marat? All right.

M. SAFIN: Thank you very much.
