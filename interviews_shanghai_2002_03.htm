2002 Tennis Masters Cup, Shanghai, China
November 14, 2002
Marat Safin - Lleyton Hewitt (AUS) 4-6, 6-2, 4-6
An interview with: MARAT SAFIN

QUESTION: You know Lleyton very well. You beat him last week; he beat you this week. What makes him the No. 1 in the world for the second year in a row? What's his big strength?

I can't say that he has unbelievable serve. I can't say that he has unbelievable volley. He's fast, yeah, but there's other people are fast, but right you said, mentally strong and consistence. I cannot talk for myself, but we cannot make it for this kind of players, they cannot play the whole time like this, for some reasons, nobody is perfect, but so that's why Lleyton is No. 1.

MARAT SAFIN: For Santoro, I'm not sure he's playing, but in everything is the part of the joke and the rest is truth, you know? But definitely I will have to just ?? I will take a few days off because I had a tough week. Doesn't matter how confident you are, but just losing three matches on basically your surface, it's tough, and so just few days off, you know, forget about tennis, so you be ambitious again and you'll be motivated again and start to be able to start to practice again, prepare yourself for Davis Cup. But still, you know, like there's some things that you need, I think. I need a few days off and afterwards I would need a long vacation to forget about this year and just to be able to start the new year with another motivation and just to be able to fight again and travel again for 11 months and win tournaments and win matches week after week.

She lost the motivation. She lost the game. She doesn't ?? she has something that ?? she needs to find something to be able to come back and be able to play big tournaments and for me, it's very important. I mean, for her is very important to stay, you know, to play tennis, enjoy the life and it would be better for her because she's only 16 years old and she has a long way to go and has a long career and you don't have to rush.

QUESTION: And the mixed?

MARAT SAFIN: I'm getting old, she's too young, so it doesn't work, you know, not yet.

QUESTION: It's a long season. You look tired, particularly you've had to play a lot of matches with Davis Cup. I'm wondering if you think that like the Ryder Cup in golf, that Davis Cup should be staggered, played every other year as opposed to the format it is now.

MARAT SAFIN: How was it before? Like Ryder Cup.

QUESTION: Or do you think it should be played every year like it is now?

MARAT SAFIN: I think it's a good question. It's a good idea, actually, because as you can see, that we're travelling for 11 months a year and if you're in the finals of Davis Cup, you don't have ?? you finish ?? basically you finish 1 December, right, and you have to fly to Australia and I mean if playing first tournament of the year is starting on 26 December, I mean there is no time for rest, there is no time for preparation. So basically they destroy the preparation because you need to prepare yourself for the big tournament like Australia for a new season. You need to be fit, otherwise you can get injured. So which is you have I think the ATP, they should do something. They should take care of the players because I think it's a good idea, you know, to make it or to make the Davis Cup a little bit earlier so we have time, you know, so we need time to rest. We cannot just play tennis. We're not machines here. We're not robots. We have also ?? I need to be with my family. I didn't see my family since four months. Can you believe it? And when I'm going to see? I'm going to see three days in France for Davis Cup and then I have to go for vacations. I want to rest, you know.

QUESTION: So you think it's a good idea?

MARAT SAFIN: That's great idea, I think. You know, is good idea, but idea stays idea. It has to become something, right? The idea is great.

Okay, I lost the match, but still I had my chance. I could have ?? I should have won, that's my mistake. That's okay, you know, but it happens. But in the first two matches, I didn't ?? I couldn't ?? I didn't finish the point, you know, I thought the point is finished, but it wasn't finished. That's why and I was getting by surprised every time I was going to the net. I didn't wait for the answer from other players, from Costa and Moya.

QUESTION: You're very charismatic player, but you lost three matches in a row. Will you come back to Shanghai?

MARAT SAFIN: Thanks a lot, but if you make the Masters next year, yeah, why not?

QUESTION: Will you leave Shanghai earlier now? When are you leaving?

MARAT SAFIN: I have to go to see my parents. I'm missing them. I'm flying tomorrow. I have to fly tomorrow to see my parents before I'm going to prepare myself for the Davis Cup.
