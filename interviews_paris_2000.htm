11/10/99 Marat Safin after losing Paris' final


courtesy Masters Series - Paris - Official site


Marat Safin lost his first Tennis Mastzers Series final in Paris, defeated 76(1), 6-1, 4-6, 6-4 by World No.1 Andre Agassi. Here are his thoughts after the match.

Q. What lessons did you learn from the match today?

MARAT SAFIN: A lot of things that each player will take from Andre Agassi: his footwork, how he's fighting, of course the speed of the ball, it's going very fast.

Also the attitude on the court. It's very difficult to play against him because you are always under pressure. If you are not 100% conditions, it's difficult to play against him. Like happens today, the first set I made a break, and it's gone so easy that you can't play like this against Andre in this moment.

When you have a break, you have to stop, you have to think, play each point. If not, is going very fast and you are not under control because you start to play on his game and you are in big trouble.

"If the people enjoy it, it's enough for me"

Q. The fact that you had a long, long match yesterday into the evening, was that still a problem for you when you started today?

MARAT SAFIN: I think no. I think the problem was that I start to play his game: long points and I was running all the time. My game is playing very short points, but I couldn't do it. First of all, I was very nervous, my first final in Mercedes SuperU 9 , also against Andre Agassi. There is a lot of respect to him.

I didn't do so badly - not so badly. I wasn't sure. You have to be sure in your conditions that you can make it. I wasn't sure, so that's why it's just gone.

The first set was actually not mine, but it was a good chance to just take my serve each time and you can finish the set 6-4, 7-5, whatever, it doesn't matter.

This is some things that make the big difference from the players of Top 20 and the players out of Top 20, this small difference. Just important points, important games. You should stop. Just a few things I have to learn, all the players has to learn, if you want to be on Top 20.

Q. But looking back on this week, will you regard this as a big step forward in your career, gives you the chance to take a big step forward, put it that way?

MARAT SAFIN: No, I think just for me I come back because I was 23 in the beginning of the year, I was playing my best tennis. After the Davis Cup against Germany, it left. I couldn't fight when they start the clay court season, Barcelona, Monte-Carlo , Hamburg , Rome , everything. I couldn't play. I felt uncomfortable on the court. I felt so slow.

The points, they left, and I went almost to 40. It's difficult to come back because you are losing your confidence. Each match when you're playing, when you're losing, your confidence is going each time. After, it's difficult to come back, to play your best level.

I got injured after Roland Garros. I was one month and a half I couldn't play, couldn't practice also. It was difficult time for me.

But now, at the end of the season, I know is too late, as I said before, but it's nice to finish the season playing really great tennis. I think for me is a good level, is good to finish like Top 25 or whatever, also playing good, so you can start the next year with all your confidence. I think it's very important for the players.

Q. What is your goal for the next season?

MARAT SAFIN: If I start again, my goal, I would like to win all my Grand Slams (laughter) . No, okay, I want to be in Top 20. Even I don't know what can happen tomorrow. It will be nice to start the year playing good, with a confidence, and after you can see how it's going. Because if you are confident, you can play great tennis. If you play great tennis, you win matches. If you win matches, you get more confidence. It start from a small thing, going bigger, bigger, bigger. Who knows, maybe I will win another Super 9. Things are making bigger, bigger, bigger. Who knows what can happen next year. Maybe not, I don't know. But I hope so.

Q. What was your injury after the French Open ?

MARAT SAFIN: I broke the capsule in the elbow so I couldn't take the racquet.

Q. Do you think one of the main things you can improve is consistency? It seems like last year after the French you also had a period where you had a hard time motivating; same this year. Is there anything you can do concretely to fight against these lapses?

MARAT SAFIN: Yes, of course. This is a problem for all the young players, like me, Di Pasquale, Ferrero, Malisse, all these players my age. They play one week unbelievable, and after is finished for one month or something like this, then he start again playing unbelievable.
It will come, I think. It will come next year when you get a little bit into ATP Tour, know the people inside. It's very important for us to be comfortable because when you start the career, you don't know anybody. You come just to ATP Tour, don't know anybody. You don't know the players, how they play. It's difficult.

So it's difficult also to play because it's coming from the head. Of course, it's coming with the years. When you getting older, you getting better with your head because you start to understand your life. It's a little bit philosophic, I know, but it's true (laughter). It's true, that you getting clever with the years, with the time. As soon as possible is better (laughter) .

Q. You say yesterday, "I want to fight tomorrow against Andre , and I want also to enjoy the game." Did you enjoy today?

MARAT SAFIN: When you run five times on the baseline like this, you not enjoying (laughter). You think, "When I going to finish?" You getting tired. Your face is the color of the court. The heart is going 200. I think it's a difficult moment.

Sometimes with a big shot, with a big serve, a big return, big point, of course, you are enjoy tennis.

But to enjoy tennis, you have to work. If you work, you play unbelievable tennis; you can enjoy it. But when you run on the baseline, it's not nice. Also, of course, against Andre , you have to run. That's why I'm playing tennis, because I enjoy my game.

It was a nice match. It's very important for us. The people enjoy it. I think they are happy. We didn't make five sets - I didn't make five sets. Still, I think it was a great week for the people because all the matches was perfect, three sets, very tight. If the people enjoy it, is enough for me. Also, I make the final, of course (laughter) .

Q. Do you know your next opponent in Moscow ?

MARAT SAFIN: Do you know (laughter) ?

Q. Yes.

MARAT SAFIN: Don't ask me this question. You know, Santoro . They call me, the director of the tournament, he told me, "Do you know who you play?"
I said, "Who?"
" Santoro ."
I couldn't talk I was so disappointed because I hate to play against him, really (laughter). For me is like almost, "You going to die tomorrow. How you feel?" Same as they told me I have to play against Santoro .
You will go out from the court without tennis, you will finish this match. You will not put one ball. You finish and you can't put one ball in or you play unbelievable. Normally is going I can't play tennis anymore against Santoro. I need two weeks to prepare. I need two weeks to practice to play the next match. Let's see. Let's see.

Q. What do you think about Russian tennis since the time of Andrei Chesnokov and the revolution in the other countries of eastern Europe from socialist countries?

MARAT SAFIN: It's difficult to explain. The mentality of Russians, it's very strange. Actually, very strong on the court. We know what we want and we fight. In tennis, you need the head to fight. I'm talking about small kids, they are starting their career, they are playing satellites, futures, they fight. They need somebody who can explain to them what they have to do to help them to go in the right way.

There are a lot of guys with a lot of talent. But the people who are with them, they don't have so much experience to show them, to learn them how they have to work, how they have to live, change a little bit of mentality. It's very important for the young players to be in the Top 100. As I said before, you have different countries like France, Spain, Italy , Germany, it's a little bit different. They need the people who can teach the small kids. It's very important.

I mean, I would like to have some more players in the Top 100, small kids, young kids, because they can play well, but they need some help, some advisor to show them, to learn them how they have to live first of all, and after how they have to play tennis. It's very important if you have a close person, that you can trust her or him, doesn't matter. You will be good player. But if you don't have, it's very difficult to play tennis when you are alone. You need somebody more, of course, who can teach you how to live and how to play tennis. It's very important.

Q. You played the semifinal against Australia . How do you see the final of the Davis Cup ?

MARAT SAFIN: I think it will be difficult, for sure. Philippoussis and Hewitt are good players. Hewitt , he can run like ten hours on the baseline. Doesn't matter; is not a problem for him.

Actually, for French people, for Pioline , Grosjean , they have a lot of talent, a lot. I don't know. I don't know, it's up to you. You play in France with the crowd. I think it will help you to win the Davis Cup .

Q. Do you think they should put Santoro in singles?

MARAT SAFIN: Yeah, but I'm not playing (laughter) .

Q. Do you think clay is a good choice, to play on clay?

MARAT SAFIN: Of course, because Mark is good player, but for indoors, for hard courts. On the clay, his serve is not so well, like here.
Yeah, go to play with Grosjean on clay court, you will see what happen. After five sets, you can't walk anymore on the court. I think it's very good, very good choice.

Pioline , he can play everywhere. He's a lot of talented. He's unbelievable. He's always going like this (hanging head down), "What am I doing here on the court?" There's a lot of talent in his body. He can play unbelievable tennis. I think France may have a good chance.

MARAT SAFIN: I remember two years ago, he played a Challenger in Ljubljana. He was playing against one guy. He slide on the court. He look at the court, "We sliding, huh?" I they he played only a few times on clay court. It was two years ago. He can play everywhere also because he's fast, very talented, he knows what he wants, he's a big fighter. It will be difficult also.
