Australian Open - January 25, 2002 Melbourne, Australia
Marat Safin - Tommy Haas 6-7, 7-6, 3-6, 6-0, 6-2.
An interview with: MARAT SAFIN


Q. Marat, what did you do during the rain delay?

A. I tried to recover because, I couldn't feel my legs, I couldn't move them at the beginning of the match because of the heat, because of many reasons, and I was just - thanks God it started to rain, and then I had an opportunity to make massage and all these things and take care of my legs, and it worked.

Q. You came out of that like Superman coming out of a phone booth.

A. I think it was a tactic of my coach and his friend, they told me - they explained to me very simply what I have to do and I just - I did it. I didn't make any miracles in this match, I just had to stay there and just go better on the return; and that's it, that's what I did. I started to return the serve, I started to read better the serve, and yeah, I did exactly what they said. So it was good, it was good.
Q. Does it remind you of the match against Grosjean in the US Open, or is it completely different?

A. No, it is completely different, because there the rain delay was like just two points, two or three points, and here I was losing two sets to one down, and Tommy was playing great and I couldn't - just I couldn't do anything because I couldn't move and I couldn't run. I had no reaction in the legs and I was late all the time on the point. He was all the time pushing me from the back, and then I just changed the game completely. It was 6-0, 6-2, and I think I played great. I served well and I did the right things.

Q. Do you think that that break also affected Tommy's rhythm?

A. Yes, I think so, because maybe he started to think, you know - I don't know what he had in his mind, because I didn't give him any chance, you know, after the break, and I made a break straightaway because he wasn't warm enough, Tommy, he didn't serve well and he couldn't - and there was a low percentage of his first serves, and I took this chance. Then I started to serve better and I started to return, and that's it, and changed completely the game.

Q. If it doesn't rain, do you lose?

A. Yes. I think I had no chance, no chance. I couldn't - I had no chance to win if he stays and it doesn't rain. I think I had very small chance to beat him. I need that break, and I'm glad he gave me.

Q. Before the rain, how important was the second set tiebreak, 0-3 down and then six points winning in a row?

A. It was important that I won the second set, so at least was a fight there, you know, and he had to play better and better, and then in the third set I had the break chance and I couldn't make it and I was losing all the time. I knew it and he knew it, that I had no chance to beat him because he played well, and I couldn't do anything against this. Then just suddenly it changed completely, the game. I was pushing him from the back and I was dominating completely on the court in the fourth and the fifth set. It was just - it was great, I think; I'm satisfied with my game.

Q. Marat, another hot day expected for Sunday - 35 degrees. How confident are you now of getting a bit of practice in?

A. I hope it will rain again. No, but I will try to prepare because I think maybe at least I made a mistake and I will not do this again. That's it.

Q. Can you tell us what that mistake was, Marat?

A. No, I can't; sorry.

Q. Was it not enough fluids?

A. No, it was just my thoughts, and I just spoke with my doctor; that's it. I think it just was a misunderstanding and it was just a small mistake that we did, and that's it. But for the next match, I will be perfect.

Q. Who gave you the coaching advice?

A. Amit Naor and his friend.

Q. What was the injury time out for, because I thought you were not supposed to have an injury time out for massage?

A. Why not? Why not? If you are injured or if you have something bothering you, you can call the doctor, it says in the rules.

Q. Anything?

A. Yes, they bother me, my legs. I couldn't - that's why we have the doctor, that's why we have the masseur from ATP, if somebody have something that doesn't work or something is going wrong with the body, we are allowed to bring him on court, and he helped me a little bit. He made me small massage, he put me hot cream and tried to do something to make a reaction in my muscles, because I couldn't run. I just had no power in my legs.

Q. What did he give you; vitamins?

A. Vitamins, simple vitamins, and just hot cream.

Q. Did the German player somehow surprise you this afternoon?

A. No, but he is improved a lot from last time we played, and he is a more solid player. He is serving well and he has a very solid baseline. That's the main thing, I think. He has improved a lot, that's why he is in the Top 10. He is doing well; he's in the semi-finals here, and he played great matches, and he is a tough guy.

Q. Marat, could you be more specific about - was it something you did do before the match you shouldn't have, or something you didn't do?

A. No, nothing, just with myself, I make a mistake, that's it. It is not - I don't want to - I can't explain to you, because it is just - - -

Q. A big mistake, if you can't run for three hours?

A. Yes, it is my mistake.

Q. What did you do? Can I put you on the spot?

A. I cannot tell you, my friend, sorry. It is a secret. I cannot tell. Tell me the reason why I have to tell you?

Q. Well, I was just fairly are curious, that's all?

A. I told you, I wish I could tell. For me, it is not a problem, but just - no, just no, I can't.

Q. Did you have intravenous drip of fluids or something during the break? How were your fluids out there?

A. It was perfect. I drink - I don't know how many litres of water and how many litres of vitamins. It wasn't a problem with the vitamins that I took, you know, the drinks. It wasn't the drinks.

Q. You must have been quite shocked, the fact that you had only played seven games since Monday evening and when you go out there, you couldn't run?

A. I couldn't run; it happens. It happens, and it happens when you are making mistakes. So it was a perfect example that you have to be careful.

Q. You have the big entourage watching you now; have you been partying hard, is that it, and you need a rest?

A. Me, partying? I wish. I wish I can party and I wish I can go one day out, you know, because there are some people, they talk too much. Before the match, or third round, I think, they said on the TV that I have been out until 6 o'clock in the morning, which is - I wish I can do it, you know, and I wish I can do it and then afterwards I can win the match. So I'm not going out, I'm quite professional, I'm going to bed at the right time, and that's it, I'm doing well.

Q. Is soccer your second-best sport, because you show greater skills with the ball?

A. I have to improve my soccer game, so I have a great time.

Q. With such a victory, what are you most happy about with your game?

A. That finally I could find my game and I was fighting. I was there, you know, I had no chance, and even then I stayed and I could find my game again. In the fourth and the fifth set I was playing perfectly. So it's very good, it's very positive for me to find my game at the right time, at the right moment.

Q. Did you watch the Johansson and Novak match? What do you think about Johansson?

A. No, I didn't watch the match, actually, I was busy. I was watching the movie.

Q. You were in the dressing room, so you hydrated a lot, took vitamins and rested?

A. No; massage - very important.

Q. Massage?

A. Yes, which just helped me a lot. I think the massage was the main thing.

Q. Marat, do you think now, beating Tommy Haas, with talking about the new generation coming, do you think you have made a big step now being one of the what they are calling the new bones and all that, you have made a huge step with this match?

A. Thank you. We have to do something, otherwise tennis will die, so we need more and more new guys - Federer and all these guys are doing something, they are improving, and we need a little bit of time also. It can't go faster than it is going, we need time, we need to find our game so then people will start to know us and tennis will grow up again into a big sport.

Q. That quarter-final, do you think it is a fair condition that you had to play today in very high heat and the other semis wasn't - the day before it was under other conditions?

A. I think it is fair. You have to play and these are the conditions. It's still hard, I know, but he is also playing, he is also running and coping with 33 degrees, and he is suffering also. But he was better prepared than me for this match, but then we started playing indoors.

Q. The other semi was the day before?

A. That's okay, that's no drama. I don't have anything against this. I think I have enough time, you know, to recover from this and just have one day. That's enough. I'm 21 years old and I can recover in one day. I will continue when I'm going to be 27.

Q. What are your thoughts about Johansson as a player?

A. He's a great player. I think he's a great player; he has a great serve and he has a very good baseline, so you have to stay there, you have to fight a lot and just take your chances. That is the main thing; there are no other options.

Q. Is he the type of player you like to play against?

A. No, definitely, nobody likes to play against him because he is a very complete tennis player. He has everything: he has a great serve, great return, great baseline, and he can play from the volley. So he is a Top 20 and he is going to be Top 10, I think, right now, and he deserves it. He is a very respectable player.

Q. Marat, in terms of breaking racquets, are you more calm than you used to be? Do you have fewer broken racquets now?

A. I have two in this tournament, so I'm getting better.

Q. Marat, there is no Swedish press here despite they have a finalist; but there is no Russian journalist here?

A. I know.

Q. How is the tennis rating in Russia now? Why is it that the press doesn't follow tennis very much?

A. Because we have better things to do, I think. No, we have TV over here, but also it's a long way and I don't think that many journalists like to fly to Australia. It's 27 hours so the Russians, they prefer to stay in Moscow. It's a great city.

Q. How about tennis popularity in Russia?

A. It's growing. Slowly it's growing, and we're doing well, you know. We have new guys coming - we have Youzhny, we have Davidenko, we have Stalyov, we have all these kind of players who are doing well, and we have very good juniors. I think tennis in Russia is getting better each time.

Q. Have you heard something about the Masters Cup next year?

A. Yes, I wish; it will great for us Russians, it will be perfect.

Q. Marat, last year in Bologna Yevgeny was questioning a little bit how professional you were. Now he says you are quite professional. What was the difference in this eight months?

A. Really? Kafelnikov? I think he is most - he knows everything. I think it is difficult, you know, to argue with him, so if he says that I'm more professional, then that's true.

Q. Last year he was questioning your professionalism.

A. What do you mean?

Q. He meant you were not quite professional?

A. I think he should take care of his life, and he has to be more professional than he is right now also, and he is 28 years old. But still, that's my life; I love my life, I like to enjoy it and I'm doing well, as you can see, so it's working. But Kafelnikov, of course, is always right, so it's okay. It's good.

Q. I noticed earlier both of you had ice packs around your neck and you had a kind of a stressful - I was on that side and you had a kind of stressful look on your face, but after you returned you won the first four games, you looked the same you did when I watched you at the US Open, like you was focused?

A. It was important for me after the locker room just to show that I am still there and I'm not dead and I'm going to fight, and just to show the first two games that I want to win, and that's it.

Q. After the fourth game, what ran through your mind after the fourth game?

A. Just you have to keep going, and doesn't let him find his game, and he didn't. He couldn't find it, you know, he couldn't find it in the fourth set and he couldn't make it even in the fifth. I didn't let him, just I played a little bit different. I tried to be focused, tried to not get pissed with myself and all these things, and just play against him and whatever comes, comes. And I was lucky also that I make the early break in the fourth and it gives me a lot of confidence.

Q. You weren't lucky; it was skill you had.

A. I was lucky; believe me. No, I was lucky. Thank you.
