Indianapolis 2002 reports by Denise


 



Indianapolis Saturday 10 Aug. 2002

Well first day in Indy. Weather hot and the sun shining out of a clear blue sky.
Got to the courts around 11pm and Marat was there practising hard with Arnaud Clement. Spent about an hour and a half – hitting the normal practice shots and then playing an on/off game – two others were also working on the same court – and as usual Marat was not satisfied. Once again the backhand slice volley is hitting the net each time. Had some good groundstrokes from the back of the court but there is still something not there. Marat is not relaxed with his game – he is determined but it is not coming to anything. To those who think Amit is not a proper coach – one - Marat talks to him – they sat and discussed things and Marat would try again.And secondly I have never seen Amit looking fitter – spent a lot of time also hitting up and obviously trying to get a feel for the courts and so to advise Marat.
I think the point that said it all for me was the fact that at the end Marat just walked off and didn’t stop for the fans that were there behind the barrier looking for an autograph – something that Marat does not normally do and says a lot about the fact that he is not comfortable with himself and his game.
He has always done well at Indy and lets hope that it works for him this week.
Saw Magnus Norman spend a lot of time practising as well as Xavier Malisse , YK, Fabrice Santoro this afternoon. Spoke to Ramon Delgado who should be Marat’s 2nd round opponent if he beats the Qualifier. Ramon I met in Liverpool and spoke to him in the restaurant. After Wimbledon he also had time off until Cinci where he lost to Ferrero so only his 2nd tournament after Wimbly.
All I can say is Marat once to do well – just needs to get his mind right. But he working towards it – looks fit and well apart from a bit of arm stretching across his front which he normally does these days to loosen up his back.
There is no laughter – no joking around – no play – just concentrated effort. He wants to do well and we must be positive and believe in him.

Indianapolis Monday 12 Aug. 2002

Hi from Indy. Sorry didn’t post yesterday but not much to say with regards to Marat.
It was very hot on Sunday and the same today.
Sunday Marat practised on Centre Court with Ivan Lubijcic and they were having a good workout. Got a couple of good photos, which I will have to send to Sergei later.
Anyhow Marat went to return a serve from IJ and the game was over. Sat down in the shade with Anatoly – no sign of Amit – and looked down at his stomach and seemed to be pinching a muscle. Anyhow came out again and then after a couple of returns pushed at his side – spoke to Anatoly in Russian and then walked up and said to IJ no good and stopped. Signed for a couple of fans and then went off. This was at 1pm. Tried to contact Amit later to find out what the problem was and was told he wasn’t available – to phone later.
Well today saw Marat in the Racquets area and had a chat. Seems Amit has had to fly home – some problem or other – and as Marat said – I am okay on my own!! Said his back was okay but didn’t practice today so guess he was giving it a rest. Was fine today – relaxed and we picked up on a couple of discussions we had had in Liverpool.
Was disappointing for one young fan Sarah who was there for the day with her tennis coach and a group of tennis players from their club – she had so hoped to see Marat practice and get his autograph – well guess who has the job of getting that autograph? No problem I am always happy to help.
So basically a very hot uneventful day of tennis – some good matches and some not so good.
Santoro breezed past Harel Levy so is still in the hunt and Stefan Koubek knocked out Ramon Delgado so is Marat’s opponent. Koubek is playing well but a rather hilarious point is that he should have automatically been in the main draw as the 14th seed but he forgot to enter! He entered a few days later but was too late. So decided to qualify and made it through by the skin of his teeth against Alun Jones on Sunday – took him 3 sets and nearly lost it I the final set tiebreak. Should have done it in 2 but through away his service – bit of an up and down player and wears his feelings on his sleeve – two of them together – should be fun! At one point in the 2nd set as he lost his service – he swore in German which the umpire did not understand but decided he was swearing so gave him a warning! Koubek muttered to him in German – you think you understood – you have no idea what I said and you truly think I swore!!

Tuesday in Indy

Well not much to report today especially not on Marat - didn't see him at all. We had two huge storms which put all the matches behind - spent a lot of time in Racquets reading my book & talking to people and Marat did not come to the courts at ll today as I think a lot of the other players - guess they must have gone to an indoor court to practice. Also he was not around at the hotel at about 11.20 this morning - saw Hewitt and entourage getting into a vehicle.
When tennis eventually got under away saw the most incredible doubles match between Hewitt/Draper and Kratochvil/A.Martin. Went to 3 sets and the second set and the start of the 3rd were magnificent tennis. Hewitt did not have it all his own way and several times he and Draper forgot to communicate and missed shots altogether going between them. As Martin will play Hewitt tomorrow think he found out a lot of shots that Hewitt doesn't like. Hewitt/Draper were eventual winners. Then watched Haas v Norman - a great battle - Haas won in two but the tie break was fantastic. There literally was only a couple of points in it.
Tomorrow ave a full day of tennis. First up on Centre Court YK v Mario Ancic then Marat v Koubek and then Hewitt v Martin. Night match is Henman and then Hewitt/Draper in doubles. Going to be a long day.
So because have seen nothing of Marat can report nothing on if he is okay or what - keeping a very low profile and without Amit have no contact.
Will report tomorrow.

Marat 's withdrawal

Hi everyone - well thanks dan not too gutted still here.
Re Marat - I admit I had a funny feeling something was not right as he was nowhere around today and I said to christine (yes we have met up) something doesn't feel right. Anyhow when it came to the match they announced he had withdrawn. I checked with the hotel and he has checked out and left Indy. Guess he will probably drive somewhere and try and recover.
One thing - Marat does not withdraw from a tournament for any little reason so he has to have been really ill. He will also be devastated that he hasn't been able to compete as loves this tournament. So lets just wish him well and hope he recovers quickly for the US OPen.
Cheers
Dendy
