2001 Cincinnati reports by Christi



Cincinnati Tournament 09 - 15 August 2001


Thursday, August 9

I get there at around 10 and head straight for the practice courts because I know that's where some good action is. I leave and come back at 4 to see no one special practicing. Then I turn my head and see Mats Wilander, so I get excited because I know Marat will be behind him! And he is! I jump up and down because he's still in Cincinnati after his 1st round loss. I watch him talk to some practice partner (I have no idea who).

He is very expressive, talks loud, and uses his hands A LOT. Gets to practicing with this guy.
Marat is SO powerful, the most powerful guy I've ever seen, although Taylor Dent is pretty close. He hits it perfectly nearly every time and is slicing every single backhand; he doesn't hit a two-hander with topspin the entire time. He hardly ever misses, but when he does one time, he yells a few sentences loudly in Russian. I almost laugh because he sounds so funny. Mats has his 2 cute blond kids on the court, and they bounce balls with their racquets. Mats just stands back there, doesn't say much. The guys who are watching joke, "Safin is a ladies man" because of the girls who are watching him. It starts raining hard. We all scramble to get his autograph, including these girls with t-shirts that say, "We miss you Marat Safin." He smiles and chuckles at their shirts and is signing and taking pictures quickly to get out of the rain. I ask some guy to take a picture of me with Marat, and ask, "Marat, can you take a picture with me?" He is taking a picture with someone else, so he says in his thick accent, "Yes, get in the picture." It's pouring rain and none of us is prepared for the picture. So I vow to get another one tomorrow. I say, "Thank you very much" and he says, "Sure, no problem." I say, "Good Luck in Indianapolis," and I'm pretty sure he says, "Thank you much," which is very funny in his accent!

Friday, August 10

My whole family gets to the ATP tennis center at 10:30, right when the matches start, so I miss out on most early practice sessions. My brother and I go to the practice courts at 3 p.m. in hopes of seeing someone. Sure enough, there is a crowd in front of the last court, so I figure it's someone big. It's Marat, which makes me so happy! We watch his long practice, and he hits with Mats, who is still really good. Marat only gets mad once, where he takes a ball and hits it about 100 feet away from the court. I don't think anyone volunteers to get it. It doesn't matter because they get free tennis balls anyway. He says, "My forehand is so bad, the
worst ever." It's great, but his slice backhand makes no mistakes at all. He comes in to volley a lot and is getting good up there. He serves really hard. Mats proposes a serve-and-volley drill, but Marat replies, "I'm bad!"

They discuss how far Indianapolis is from Cincinnati, and Marat says it's 116 miles, so Mats says it should take about 3 hours or less. I almost touch him through the fence, but that's a serious invasion of privacy.
Marat's trainer is Russian, I think, so he tells Mats that Marat is "???" (I don't know what the word was, fill in if you know the word!), which means tired in Russian. Marat finally comes out. All of these kids ask for his autograph. I say, "Mr. Safin" for him to sign the other side of our Russian nesting doll, which has already been signed by Yevgeny Kafelnikov. I thought he would laugh or something, but he's not so cheerful today. I ask if he can take a picture with me, if it's not too much trouble, and he says sure. I don't think he remembers me from yesterday. He is shirtless (quite skinny now, not as buff as was a few years ago) and holding his bag and puts his arm on my back. I put my arm around him but end up hitting his bag, so I don't get to feel his sweaty back. My brother takes the picture, and I'm so excited! Marat takes pictures with everyone and signs for all. He's very nice to his fans. We follow him all through the grounds, right after I ask Mr. Wilander for his autograph. It's very easy to get his autograph because all of the kids who want Marat's autograph don't know who Mats Wilander is. Marat walks near the concession stands, shirtless and towering over everyone, while everyone stares and whispers. It's hard to miss a 6'4" shirtless Russian carrying a tennis bag with a trainer and a coach! The little kids run to get his autograph, and he signs for all of them. He ends up on the golf course goofing around with Mats and his trainer. I wish I could have followed him more, but that's stalking. It was so fun today!

Saturday, August 11

Nearly all of the players, except today's semifinalists, have cleared out of Cincinnati. I am sure Marat has left for the RCA Championships in Indianapolis, which is 3 hours away, because he and Mats were discussing their trip yesterday. That's ok, I had a great time watching Marat Thursday and Friday and hope that he will do well at Indianapolis!


Christi
