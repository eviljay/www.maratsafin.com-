Paris 2001 reports by Denise





Paris, Tuesday 30 October 2001
Hi everyone from Paris. Well didn’t do a report last night as we finished so late and we were so tired just didn’t have the energy. What has been happening? Monday must be known as the day of tiebreaks! There seemed to be so many of them and it made the matches so long! The last match of Philipoussis/Malut started at 11.10pm – well only stayed for three games and then went back to the hotel.

Our hotel is right next to POBP so just walk out the door and up the stairs and we are there. It is an older hotel but in the same group as the hotel next door and the one the main players are staying in which is about ten minutes away. At our hotel all the camera crew and officials e.g. linesmen etc are staying so quite a laugh. Quite a few South Africans amongst the cameramen so I have been having a good laugh with them. In fact the one guy recognized me from being on the same flight out of Stuttgart so we went from there! Getting too well known that is the problem!!

Well apart from the incidentals – back to the tennis. Saw some good matches and will give you my thoughts on them in a minute. Went to see yesterday morning if Marat had arrived but by the time we left at 12.30pm he still hadn’t come in from St. Petersburg. Had seen Schalken arrive from Stockholm and Henman had already arrived from Basel. Ilaria had fun – had her photo taken with Haas and Roddick and Ivanesevic and Ferrero.
I am a great photographer these days!

Was cold standing around but great to see all the players. Watched Lapenti defeat Dupuis despite vociferous support from the crowd for the Frenchman. Lapenti fought back well and that has been proved today with his beating Hewitt. Didn’t see that match but Chris did and she said that it appeared that Hewitt had a stomach bug or something and did not feel well. Then saw Arazi take out Kiefer – Kiefer just could not cope with Arazi’s spin – think Santoro against Arazi will be quite something – see who can soft touch and outspin the other!! Then Rusedski had no answer to Malisse who had one of his better days – when he plays well he is very good and when he doesn’t he is horrid! Wonder what tomorrow will bring?

Then of course had to watch Pioline/Kucera to see who Marat would be playing. Of course the crowd was all for Pioline and we had one lone supporter close to us who was shouting for Kucera. Ilaria and I were divided as to who we wanted to win – thought it would be better with Kucera as his game is similar to Marat’s and he would avoid the rather “over the top” support of the French crowd for Pioline. Kucera was not very complimentary about the crowd in his talk after the match – said they should remember the difference between football and tennis!!

As I said before the matches had been so long that we didn’t wait to see the end of the last match. This morning started off early and went to the players hotel to wait for Marat. It was cold with a biting wind and we stood and waited!! Saw the other players come out – there were a few others waiting – one young girl was over the moon when Mark Philipoussis signed her poster and kissed her on the cheek – tears galore!! As usual Juan Carlos Ferrero is very good at signing and letting everyone take pictures of him and with him – very obliging and always smiling.

Then suddenly Marat was coming out – said Ilaria he is here and we rushed over and I took a photo of Ilaria with Marat and then she took the photo of me with Marat – pic attached. I am here I promise. Didn’t get any other pics of him as too many people around and Ilaria had my camera and she was over the moon!!

Then just after Marat went off to practice – Mats arrived from the airport so he IS here. Later went and had a coffee inside the hotel and Mats was sitting talking to someone right close by. Watched the players going by and enjoyed the atmosphere!! Then went off to watch the tennis. I watched doubles first have got on talking terms with the South African Ellis Ferreira so went to watch him and Rick Leach win their game.

And then to Court 2 to watch the end of Schalken beating Luzzi – never mind Ilaria! Then we watched Ljubicic take out Moya – he is playing very well indeed. Saw Ferrero watching the play for a bit and Ivanesevic was watching as well. He had been beaten earlier – don’t think he was really worried now he has qualified for Sydney - think he is saving his shoulder! Then Ferrero came on to play Portas.

Court 1 is really great you are so close to the players in fact Ilaria was right in front behind the players and I was behind her and Ferrero kept walking right up to the edge and toweling himself and hanging the towel over the side of the stand – so close you could touch him! Why can’t Marat play on a court like that! Imagine!! Then back to Centre Court to watch Enqvist/Escude and Keurten/Uhlirach – Keurten seems to have got his confidence back – made an easy match of it. See there was another upset in that Corretja lost to C. Rochus!

Earlier I had met up with Chris and we watched the doubles together and then she joined Ilaria & I to watch the last two matches together and then we came back to the hotel and had a drink . She had spoken to Blandine earlier and hopefully we will meet up tomorrow as well as Slestat will be there tomorrow too so should be fun. We have a very long day – Marat is last on Centre Court and he hates playing so late!! Hope Mats can keep him focused. We are all deciding what should happen to the other threats to Marat so he better do his part while we will put the “bad” thoughts on his other opposition to Sydney!

It is fun being here – POBP is a bit disorganized but is situated in a good area with lots of small restaurants and bars around so makes for a festive time. Now nearly midnight and I must finish this and send off to Sergei. Will try and get some more photos tomorrow of Marat and send off to you all. Goodnight and we will have sweet dreams of our favourite man. I am sure Ilaria will!!


Paris, Wednesday 31 October 2001
Actually it is now the 1st November – it is 1am and I am trying to get this
written.

What a night – what a day!!
First of all spent a long time waiting for Marat today and eventually saw him at 13.45 - more photos – and he was very relaxed and happy and said he would play well tonight!!
Then off to the tennis. Spent most of the day on the Court No.1 – as I said last night – the players are so close – you can touch them. Wouldn’t it be wonderful if Marat played there!! Their programming is crazy – today we had Federer against Novak – and Novak won!! Yeah!!! Then Costa against Canas – brilliant rallies and Court 1 makes it all seem so alive – the atmosphere is incredible. Then after that had the two Swedes Enqvist and Johansson – another base line rally game brilliant tennis and Johansson came out the winner!! So far it was fantastic.

Then everyone was trying to get into the court to watch Philipoussis against Kafelnikov – not enough room while upstairs on Centre Court – Boutter knocked out Henman and Santoro and Arazi played a tame match and then Grosjean against Hrbaty – because they are French they were playing to a fairly empty stadium – and people were queuing up to get into Court 1!! No sense in that lot. We left when Kafelnikov broke Philipoussis in the 2nd set and went upstairs. Ilaria needed to eat and I went to find Chris. She had seats right in the front so went to sit with her.

Then Blandine joined us and we put up Ilaria’s sign “Good Luck Marat” and watched. It is a wonder I do not get a heart attack I get so nervous and he kept us right on the edge. The confidence was not totally there - not enough first serves though was getting better towards the end and the humour – well that was hilarious. The umpire had caused great consternation between Philipoussis and Kafelnikov on Court 1 – overruling on the far side and they kept asking him was he sure he could see that and then Ilaria and I couldn’t believe it – he was umpiring Marat’s match. Well at one point in the 3rd set Marat asked him if he was sure that serve was okay?? You are sure?? No I am not being cheeky I am just asking to make sure you are happy!! Very tongue in cheek!!

Then in the first set where he was getting cross and the crowd made a noise so Marat got down on his knees and bowed to the court to say sorry. Also said “Sorry” loudly another time. Lots of humour – points of brilliance and other times total mess up – just hope that this was the start like Indy against Sa and then he got better and better! He had better because I don’t think my nerves can stand it!! Mats was there as well as Amit and Gerrard.

As we did last night came back to the hotel to have a drink and then look at tomorrows schedule.
Well cannot believe it – our wish has come true – we have Marat on Court No. 1
after 4.30 pm – he will be so close and we will be there to watch him. Guess you will not have it on TV as Keurten is playing Schalken at the same time on Centre Court. Today Haas won – maybe he gets knocked out tomorrow ? – Henman out, Kafelnikov through – Roddick out – a few upsets.

“Well he has started and tomorrow it is Vinciguerra – Marat you can do it – we believe in you but please try and do it a bit easier don’t think our nerves can stand it!!”


Paris, Friday 02 November 2001

Well the bubble has burst and the tennis year is over for Marat. A year I think he should put down to experience and look forward to 2002. Last night he pressed the self destruct button and really did that. At one point Marat said “ I never play such BAD tennis before”. He knew it – we knew it – nothing would go right. Maybe it all started at the hotel yesterday at 12 when he came out – Mats said he was too tired to sign – too early and Marat looked tired. I jokingly asked him if he was too tired to sign and he said no I thought you want photo so then he signed but not as relaxed as the day before.

Court 1 was packed – we got there at 1pm and sat through Johansson/Lapenti – Lapenti had no answer to Johansson and then I was the lone voice shouting for my compatriots Wayne Black/Kevin Ullyett of Zimbabwe as they played Max Mirnyi/Sandon Stolle and they won in 3 sets. I was very excited. Actually a lot of the players looked jaded yesterday – like they really had been out enjoying the Paris nightlife and didn’t really want to be playing tennis. Keurten out and also Ferrero – just had no answer to Arazi and looked really “sluggish” in the third set. From the start of the game Marat just didn’t look confident – was rushing things – broke Vinciguerra but then promptly lost his serve – broke his racquet in the first set – hit it hard first looked at it and then decided he better make a good job of it so well and truly smashed it. And the warning followed. Didn’t even shout or anything – just looked very resigned – was not even running properly – in fact the main problem was he was playing Vinciguerra’s game and waiting for him to make the mistakes. Instead Marat made them – lots of them! Didn’t vary his game at all except towards the end of the second set and then started looking better but it was too late – Vinciguerra had the break and he was feeling confident and despite a couple of close calls Marat could not break him back and that was that! A very upset Marat – threw it all away. One of the cameramen (from South Africa) staying in the hotel said when he came into the bar for a drink last night – your man lost – I said yes he did a self destruction exercise. He said they had Tommy Haas’ coach Red doing the commentary on the match and he was very excited that Marat lost as that confirmed Tommy would probably go to Sydney. So the year is over.

As Marat had said previously - forget this year – 2002 he will be better and fit and well. He now has a good break to relax – sort out his affairs and get himself into shape for Australia. Don’t know if he is playing Hopman Cup this year or going to Adelaide – hopefully we will be able to check out details later. As to Mats – I don’t know – there was an interview in the daily programme which was at 20.00 hours on Wednesday before Marat’s first match – and Mats said basically he likes working with Marat – he is very talented but whether they will continue or not he doesn’t know. My opinion – Marat needs to sort out a proper team around him that can give him the support he needs – next year he is 22 – another year older and wiser – and time to get his life together. Unfortunately the learning curve is a harsh one. We all have the bad times and then the good times come again. This was a bad time for Marat – I am sure he has learnt a few lessons in the realistic world this year and now he needs to apply those lessons learnt to the New Year 2002.

He has the talent and he has shown he can think – now he must combine the two to become the lethal talent we know he is. Rest, recuperate, relax – the three R’s Marat and then next year starts with a bang. You will be No. 1 again – but build to it slowly next year – not all in a rush! Well I hope to visit a few more tournaments next year. I have met a lot of the fellow fans and it has been great. I hope to meet more next year. We all come from different parts of the world but we have one thing in common – MARAT SAFIN.
Long may he play and long may he reign!


Paris Friday 2 November 2001

So Friday started – woke tired and a little bit down – always find that once the andrenalin stops pumping – feel very deflated. Slept a bit later and then decided once had had breakfast and sent off Thursday report, that would go along to the Sofitel and see if Marat was still there as he would be leaving today. Got to the hotel about 11.30 and met with Aurelie and Cindy and the rest of the crowd who were normally in front of the hotel.

Dedicated band we are – waiting in the cold – laughing and chatting just to catch a glimpse of Marat! Had arranged that as the tennis was only starting at 2pm – we would meet with Chris at 1pm and go for lunch before going on to watch tennis.

Ilaria and I had some coffee and then went outside to join the others. Aurelie said “Look Marat’s car” and there was this gorgeous dark blue Mercedes Coupe – very sleek and low slung with Monte Carlo licence plates – definitely suits the image and the man – I am definitely in love with that car – but then I am a sucker for beautiful sports cars!! Helps as well that the driver is so sexy as well!! Not sure of the model – did look at is but it is the top of the range Mercedes Coupe.

Well Ilaria and I hung around and he had not appeared and it was now 1.05pm so had to go to meet Chris at our hotel. Walked fast and then when got to the hotel – Chris says “We must go back” so fast walk back again to the Sofitel and introductions all round. Chris was very pleased to be there so she would actually see Marat with all his fans.

About 1.50pm I saw through the glass front of the hotel Amit heading towards the desk with Marat following. “He’s coming everyone!!” Well there were a whole lot of fans inside all mobbing him and then out he came. Looked a bit sleepy – always does when it is early – and 2pm is early for Marat! Before that the bags had come out and been loaded in the car – 2 large Dunlop bags, 1 suitcase, large BOSS carry all plus 2 others – good thing that the Merc has a large boot.

Marat was carrying a small bag and Amit a racquet which while everyone was crowding round Marat I spoke to Amit and got given the racquet because it had a broken string! In the meantime Chris was taking photos and Ilaria had managed to kiss Marat so a lot was happening.

Then Marat managed to get to the car and open the door and saying “I must go now I have a long way to drive” and another one tries to have a photo and kiss him – oh the popularity!!

While this was all happening Amit was at the passenger side and says doesn’t anybody want to take my picture with my car?? So I laughed and said but I have taken your picture before and he smiled and I took another one. Pays to keep the manager happy!!

Then Marat climbed into the car, said goodbye and closed the door and drove off to Monte Carlo!! Everyone was talking at once and wanted to touch the racquet! Plain no name nothing on the strings for everyone’s info – just a small yellow sticker which said Safin – the stringers reference obviously!!

So we said goodbye to our friends and then went in to have lunch in the Sofitel. Had a lovely lunch and saw Max Mirnyi and father – when we left Max was sleeping in a chair in the lobby while dad sorted out the bill!

The rest of the day saw the tennis until late as had to watch the doubles Zim playing SA/US and ZIM lost in a tight 3 setter. Nearly 1am again before got to bed – lots of late nights!! But before that had a real downer. Before went to the tennis – went past our hotel and took the racquet into the room and put it into the wardrobe.

Well to cut a long story short – when we came back in the early hours – no racquet in the wardrobe and a new Masters Series T Shirt that Ilaria had bought was also gone! Went down and reported it to the desk but guess that is it. Not sure how it happened – they will investigate it today but that is that! Now we can understand how Marat lost his passport/chain etc - don’t understand how anyone even knew.
So for a brief while I had Marat’s racquet in my hand!!

Today it is the semi finals – intriguing prospect – Kafelnikov against Vinciguerra who played very well indeed against Schalken and then the crunch match for the Masters Cup – Haas against Grosjean. It will be interesting to see how Haas will cope with the French crowd – they get very carried away.

So Marat goes to Monte Carlo to plan his season and decide what to do about his coach and where to go on holiday. He is not going to Hopman Cup and will play Auckland instead before Aus Open. That is definite from his manager.

So for once we have news from”the horses mouth” so to speak Glad we went to the hotel – that car was very worth seeing apart from the owner/driver of course!
