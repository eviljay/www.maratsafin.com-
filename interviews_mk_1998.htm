November 1998 - From the Russian newspaper "Moskovski Komsomolets"
An interview with Marat just before the Kremlin Cup in ’98.
He went out in the first-round 6-3 6-4 to Paul Haarhuis.


Question - Marat, what is your mood right now?

Marat Safin - Well, what can I say...For the past two weeks I've only appeared 5 or 6 times on court. I had some problems with my stomach. But now, thank God, it is back to normal. I don’t think that this induced downtime will stop me from playing well in the Kremlin Cup.

Question - What does ‘well’ mean for you?

Marat Safin - To get through as many rounds as possible. That is my goal for every competition. And that is why I never say to myself: "Whatever it takes I have to get to the semi-finals or the final!" If this is the goal you set yourself, you’ll be putting the racket back into the bag right after the first-round.

Question - Who don't you want to play at the start?

Marat Safin - This year very strong players have come here - Kafelnikov, Peter Korda, Goran Ivanisevich. As for me I don't care who I play. I don't feel any diffidence. If I’d been afraid of people there’s no way in the world I’d have been able to get from the 210th to 50th during one season.

Question - Yeah, you've caused quite a stir this year. Take the French Open for example. It even seemed for a while that they were talking about you more than they were about Kafelnikov.

Marat Safin - I have the greatest memories from Roland Garros. I was very glad to beat Andre Agassi and then the Champion Gustavo Kuerten. I was simply working at myself a lot because in that particular case anything was possible. My rise is not accidental. I guess I can call this year fantastic. Aspiration and luck combined together helped me to make this spurt. In the beginning of the year I had no money to pay for the travelling. Now I am the only one who supplies myself with money.

Question - So, you’re 100% happy with your results this year?

Marat Safin - Not quite. After France I had a lousy spell. I lost in first rounds of 6 tournaments in a row. It wasn’t because I thought of myself as a star. Even now I don’t think of myself as one. It was just some psychological exhaustion, I lost my shape. By the way I think I mentioned that I always play badly in summer, when in November ,according to the last year, anyway, I do OK.

Question - Well that's easy to understand: the spirit wants some rest...

Marat Safin - Tennis players don't have much time left for recreation. Trips and tournaments non-stop, living in hotels...You work really hard during training, no hanging out. But to be honest, sometimes I really don't feel like hitting the ball on the court - I am so lazy. I only really get time to rest at the end of the year. Right now I am going to spend some time in Moscow. The best vacations are with parents. I only had a chance to see them for two months maximum this year. I only communicate with them by phone. I try to call them every two days.

Question - How do you spend your spare time?

Marat Safin - Sometimes I feel like reading a book or playing computer games. At times I manage to go to a club. I like listening to music and I like different types of it: Metallica, Halloween, Enigma. I should say that the hours of rest I have are based completely on my mood, and my mood changes 10 times a day!

Question - And what about if you are in a bad mood? What is the solution then? Marat Safin - Women! (laughter) There should always be a person that can help you out with advice and things. Of course your parents but in the most part I think it falls to the coach.

Question - Having spoken about women, do you have a steady relationship with somebody?

Marat Safin - Yes.

Question - Among the WTA players, who’s your favourite?

Marat Safin - I won't be original: Anna Kournikova. She is a very beautiful girl or young lady - whichever you’d call her. I also like the German Jana Kandarr, she is more a model. But I like Anna and Jana more outwardly - from afar. For example, I wouldn't marry Anna.

Question -So you’re saying that you’re already thinking about getting married?

Marat Safin - As they say in one movie: "It can never be too early or too late to think about it." But I am not ready yet to have a family of my own, I am only 18. But closer to the age of 25, that would be more the right time. During this period I need to feel like I’m completely a man, to think about myself that way and to become a man. To achieve something, to be able to take care of two people... At the moment I’m only moving in this direction.

Question - A formal suit always fits a real man...

Marat Safin - Well, well, well... Here you go again remembering the Davis Cup. Well, I haven't bought myself a suit yet, I haven't. Despite that thing that I was jammed into then. In fact, I decided a long time ago: I should buy one. I keep telling myself every day: "That's it, today I'm going to the store." And then I think: what if I go tomorrow? And there I go: tomorrow, tomorrow... I have already told you what a lazy guy I am. Though...when I come to Spain in the end of the month I’m going to buy myself a suit. For sure!

Question - What about football? Are you going to see the game "Spartak" vs. "Real"?

Marat Safin - Actually I was thinking about it. I’ve been a Spartak fan since I was a kid. I was sorry I didn’t see their game with "Inter". It is a pity that nobody scored. By the way, if I wasn’t a tennis player, I would probably like to become a football player. Though, what a football player would I have been when I am so tall!? I lack co-ordination for football!

Question - And what is it that you lack in tennis?

Marat Safin - I need to work on my serve. Right now it seems to be my main weapon, the thing that can make me win. I have got big plans! Next year I want to get into the Top Ten. Maybe I could even carry a big stick at the first racket?...
