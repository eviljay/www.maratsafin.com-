November 19, 2000
M. SAFIN - Mark Philippoussis 3-6, 7-6(7), 6-4, 3-6,7-6(8)
An interview with: MARAT SAFIN

Marat Safin defeated Mark Philippoussis 3-6, 7-6(7), 6-4, 3-6 and 7-6(8) in the singles final.

MARAT SAFIN: I'm really sorry, but can we make it quite fast, because I have to go to the hospital. I have to make some points.

Q. Congratulations. Who do you fancy next, Tyson or Lennox Lewis?

MARAT SAFIN: What?

Q. Who do you fancy playing next, Tyson or Lennox Lewis.

MARAT SAFIN: I don't have my humor somewhere, but not here.


Q. It was a very tight match, very close finish, but you came through it. We're all wondering now if you feel that you're No. 1, even though the ATP Tour is still sort of sticking to the race and not counting the entry system?

MARAT SAFIN: I think, I mean, at the end of the year, it's coming. It's not the beginning of the year where you're just starting, you're No. 1. After the first week of the year, I think it's the end of the year. I just show it to everybody that I can win good tournaments. I'm not lucky, nobody is - has been injured. Nobody has any problems. Everybody played this year. So I think I - why I have not to think? Why I cannot think that I am No. 1? I think I'm playing great tennis, and I won enough tournaments to show it on all the surface - not on grass, but I'm looking forward. I think I am just - at this moment I am No. 1. Sorry, but maybe somebody don't like it, but that's life.

Q. Going to the hospital, will you need stitches in the wound, do you think? Have you been told?

MARAT SAFIN: I think I have to make - I don't know, points. Stitches.

Q. Stitches. You have to have stitches?

MARAT SAFIN: Yeah. I have to, because I open my - I hit it with the frame of the racquet. So I have to take care of myself also.

Q. It seems you were knocked down on the court. Can you explain how?

MARAT SAFIN: How it happened and what happened after?

Q. Yeah.

MARAT SAFIN: All the story? (laughter) Just it was I tried to make - I don't know - to hit the ball. Everybody saw it. I don't know how, but I hit it with the frame. In the first seconds, you start to understand because it's - it hurts a lot. You get hit, and you just don't want to see your arms, because you know that you're bleeding for sure. You can feel it. Then you get - just you don't understand what is happening. When you see the blood, you get scared. That's it. After, I just - you have to think. I start to think too much about my - what is happening with my - how you say the name? What is this? (pointing to eyebrow)

THE MODERATOR: The injury.

MARAT SAFIN: Not the injury.

Q. Eyebrow.

MARAT SAFIN: Yeah. You start to think what I have to do. Because if it gets open and I have to finish the match, I have to quit, because it's not possible to get any more help from Alex. So I have to say, "Thank you," and, "Bye-bye." That's, you know, after two sets, winning two sets, you just don't want to give it away.

Q. It seems that you started the first set a little bit slow today, then you enter the match in the tiebreak of the second. How come? Were you tired?

MARAT SAFIN: No, I think I had probably too much respect. I was maybe not warm enough. I was thinking too much, because I saw him, I saw his matches, and he impressed me very much. But after the match, we start to forget about all these things and we start to play the game more or less. It was going better and better each time. But also, I'm not satisfied with my return, because if you play against these guys and you don't return, there's no chance to beat them. But today, I was lucky. But next day, it won't be like this, because it was in tiebreak. And tiebreak, as you know, it is a lottery. You never know what is gonna happen. These guys, you have to put them under pressure. They have to be under pressure all the time on their serves. So it's - I have to improve this thing.

Q. How close was it to a retirement today?

MARAT SAFIN: It was quite - very close. Because I asked Alex, and he said if it's - we were out of three minutes. It's only three minutes. If it starts bleeding, he can count to put something - some glue. But if it starts bleeding, I have to stop the match, because it's already is not fair for the opponent because he has to wait all the time. You know? It's not the point. The guy is running like a dog, and he's fighting. Because of other guy is bleeding, you have to wait all the time. I think is not correct. I was very close.

Q. Your victory in the US Open is regarded as your best performance ever. How close to that one was this one?

MARAT SAFIN: Very. It's different. Different game. Because I didn't have to lose anything. When I came to the US Open, I was 9 in the world. Here, I am No. 2, and everybody is looking at you, and they can - you're No. 1 next week. So everybody is expecting that you gonna win. Mark played great tennis. I was very close to lose it. And just I think quite confidence that the people are satisfied with my game. I think I deserved it, to be No. 1 next week. And also thanks to all the people who came today. I think they helped me a lot.

Q. Do you think the injury helped you a little bit, because you broke him for the first time right after that.

MARAT SAFIN: No. But maybe with that point, yes. But during all other three sets, he thinks too much. You know, a lot of energy is going out because you are not concentrated on the match. You are thinking about what is happening with your head, and how if it is bleeding or not. It's a situation you do not know. You know that after the match you have to go to the hospital; they have to put some... whatever.

THE MODERATOR: Stitches.

MARAT SAFIN: Stitches. And you get scared. Of course, you can see the doctor putting the stitches. You get afraid. I'm a human being, so it's a little bit - I know the match, since that time I start to think about this. And I don't like this situation.

Q. And in the last tiebreak, what made the difference?

MARAT SAFIN: What made the difference?

Q. Experience or...?

MARAT SAFIN: Not experience. Experience, if you have enough experience, you don't miss - I don't lose five matchpoints like you going first time to the school. So easy balls. I mean I was just lucky. I was lucky that he missed the last - his forehand was quite easy. Thanks to him, I won this match. But not to me, I was just trying to do all my best. But I had my opportunities. I didn't, you know, do anything special. I miss many easy balls. So... Thanks to him to help me.

Q. Do you think that the racquet took a revenge against you like last week in St. Petersburg?

MARAT SAFIN: The racquet? Now in this case the head.

Q. What will you do until Lisbon? Are you going to rest?

MARAT SAFIN: Of course I have to rest, because I am tired. The club on the side, I'll be 100 percent concentrated on the matches, I run a lot. It's a lot of energy you're spending on the matches. After the match, because you're still in the match, playing the match. And I need to rest because I don't want, you know, I'm playing okay, and I have to be calm and to be prepared in Lisbon. That's the week that you have to be 100 percent.

Q. It means that the Masters Cup is a huge goal for you, or...?

MARAT SAFIN: Well, fighting for to finish the year No. 1. I mean, everybody gonna expect from me something more than I can do. You know, they want to see the No. 1. So, I will try to do my best, but also, it's, you know, it's important for me going there, to show to everybody I know how to play tennis. And I'm not a yo-yo from Moscow, a guy who doesn't know anything. I just have to prepare there. Because the last week of the year, eight best players in the world, and we'll see what I'll be able to do there.

Q. Among all the titles you got this year, apart from the US Open, where would you put this one?

MARAT SAFIN: I think after US Open. Right after. Thanks, guys. Sorry, really.
