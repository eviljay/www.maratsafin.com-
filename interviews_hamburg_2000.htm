05/21/00 Interview: Kuerten and Safin on Sunday during Tennis Master Series 2000 in Hamburg



After the singles final, Gustavo Kuerten and Marat Safin did the press conference together. Here is what Hamburg champion Kuerten and finalist Safin had to say after the match.


Q: Marat, how tired were you at the end? How frustrating was it to lose so closely, only two points away?

Safin: Well, I wasn't so tired, he was more tired because he played two finals in a row. But I was too nervous, because it was the second final of a Super-9 Masters Series, I was under pressure, because I wanted to win this event. It also took time and then you start to think at the wrong moment. I had my chance 0:30 at 6:5, I had my chance during the tie-break and that's it. That's it, because I didn't play my great tennis today. I tried to do my best and I tried to push myself to fight. Actually, he was better than me, for sure. He played an unbelievable game. If I had won it would be wrong, I think, because he's much better at the moment and it was normal that he won.

Q: Gustavo, do you think you have time to recover for Paris?

Kuerten: Yes, I think I'm right that I'm not playing next week, so I can rest and work on my body, so I'll be fit again for the French. It's an important week now. I hope to enjoy this moment first and then I'll try to think again during my recovery and then work for next week.

Q: Do you expect great things at Roland Garros after the way you played here this week and before?

Safin: Yes, because I'm surprised with my game and I didn't expect to play like this after four months without any victory. Actually there were some changes with my coach and I'm trying to play different tennis that nobody expects. But I'm looking forward to Roland Garros, it's an important tournament and it's a Grand Slam, so I'd like to be in good form there. It's a great event and it's also a lot of points in the game. So it would be great to get a good result there. I hope I'll be ready for Roland Garros, I think I'll be ready because I'll take a week of practice in Dьsseldorf. I think it would be great to get some good results in the French Open.

Q: Do you think within yourself you have the chance to win it?

Safin: Look at him.

Q: Is he your favourite?

Safin: No, because there is someone like Norman or a lot of Spanish guys like Moya and Ferrero, there are a lot of good players. You can't really say who is going to win, it's not going to be easy, so you have to work hard and be prepared for each match. You can do stupid things during the match, but you have to play from the beginning until the end. I was fighting but I couldn't win today, but what is different in the French Open is that there are 128 players and everybody wants to win. It's much tougher.

Q: Do you think it's an advantage for Guga having won it before?

Safin: I don't know, ask him. I don't know. Kuerten: No, I think not. I think everybody is there to win, like he said, it's about competition and who is going to be well prepared and who is going to be in his best day. Last year I was playing so well, I was feeling the ball so good, and then in the quarters I played one guy and he was playing better than me, so I had no chance. I'm just expecting to have a good time there.

Q: Is Chesnokov going to work with you in Paris?

Safin: I hope so, he's already in Dьsseldorf and he's a little bit upset with me and I hope he won't be angry tomorrow. But I will need him for Roland Garros, defenitely, because I need some support and also Aleksander is going to be there, I hope so. I will need Andrei, because it will be a difficult week and I will need some support from his part. Also, he knows a lot about tennis and it would be great if he would help me during this week. And afterwards we're going to talk about our future. But I need him, because he's a great player and he's a great coach.

Q: Why is he upset with you?

Safin: No, it was a joke, sorry. No, he's not upset, he's happy, because I found my game. I know what I have to do and he needs time to work with me, that's what he said, just a few weeks to work with the shots and the mental problems. I think it would be great to have a few weeks to work with him, he can explain to me everything, what I should do in each moment.

Q: Guga, what did you learn from your defeat in the French Open last year? Is it a match that is still in your mind?

Kuerten: No, actually we're used to lose, because if you play tournaments every week, and if you lose once you try to forget it. I think it shows the way tennis can be, one day can be different from the other and you have to be well prepared and wait for your opportunity.
