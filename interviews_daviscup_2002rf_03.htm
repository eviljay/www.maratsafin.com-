Dec 01, 2002, Palais Omnisports Paris Bercy, Paris, FRA
Marat Safin - Sebastien Grosjean 6-3, 6-2, 7-6 (13-11)
An Interview with Marat Safin


Could you describe your emotion on match point, just when you won the point?

MARAT SAFIN: I was praying to finish in three sets. Just when was everything finished, I was so happy. I didn't have to go to the fourth set because you never know how it can finish. He started to play well. Also, my physical conditions, I was getting tired already. I wanted to finish in three sets. I was lucky that I could make it.

What about your happiness?

MARAT SAFIN: "Happiness," what do you mean? We still have to win. It's okay to win one match. For me, for myself, it's great, but I hope it will help the team, you know, it will help to Misha to win the last match, just to win Davis Cup. Why not? It's the last point. Two Juniors are playing basically. We'll see (smiling).

Do you remember the last time you played so well and so consistently from the first to the last point?

MARAT SAFIN: Two days ago (laughter).
No, I think probably Bercy when I won here. I think I played a great match. Every match I was playing, I was full of confidence, even if I didn't make any good results before that tournament. Just, I don't know, nothing to lose. Playing outside of Russia, playing against a big team, against all the spectators, it just was great. I was inspired.

You have been pulling your team for the past three days. Can you describe what it's like? Is it difficult?

MARAT SAFIN: It's difficult. It's difficult emotionally because every match you play, it's difficult to play because you're not playing only for yourself, you're playing for the team, for the country. Everybody's watching you. They want you to win.
It's really difficult, you know, to fight against the player, against the crowd, against everything, like when you choke, which is normal, being nervous in a match, especially in the final.
But I'm happy. I want to win. It doesn't matter how tired you are, how bad you're playing, doesn't make any difference, just we want to win. It's the last match. It's also my last match of the year, it was. I'm happy to finish. I have done everything what I could do to help the team to win the Davis Cup.

How do you explain you recovered so well after the doubles yesterday?

MARAT SAFIN: Still young, youth, nothing else. I'm just 22 years old, and my body let me play so many hours without being tired. The masseur, he did a great job. Also the support, it means a lot. I mean, if it would go to five sets, to play a tough match, then it will be difficult. After two days in a row playing tough matches, emotionally and physically you are getting tired. Still, I'm 22, so it saves my life today.

What was the key of the match today?

MARAT SAFIN: Just start from the beginning playing your game without giving to Sebastien any chance to control the match, otherwise... He's a really smart player. He knows how to play in the tough moments. He knows how to read the other play player. He knows where to put the ball.
I was going for it. Why not? Some shots I had to go for it, just put pressure all the time on Sebastien, on the return, on his serve, just move him around the court, just going to the net.

You think that the easy success against him in Roland Garros helped you today?

MARAT SAFIN: Yes, of course. Last time we played in Roland Garros, I won. I just understood how I have to play against him. It also gave me some kind of confidence that I can beat him. Already I knew from the beginning what I have to do, when I have to do. I was also a little bit lucky at some point of the match.

When did you know that Kafelnikov would not play? Does it change anything in your mind?

MARAT SAFIN: Actually, from yesterday. We decided to put Mikhail because Yevgeny was tired after the doubles. He couldn't play today, so we decided to put the young player. Why not? We'll see if he can manage to win, at least show great tennis.

Did you have a conversation with Yevgeny yesterday after the doubles? Did you help him decide not to play?

MARAT SAFIN: No. We have enough people, smart people, in tennis. Shamil and some other coaches, we have Cherkasov with us, some other players, explayers, that understand tennis. They decided what to do.

Did Yevgeny volunteer not to play?

MARAT SAFIN: Yeah, because he said himself, "Whatever you decide, guys, I will respect it."

We saw Golanov beside you. How did he help you?

MARAT SAFIN: A lot. A lot of support is what I need. Some stage of the match, he gave me some good advices, not to go back in the third set, to keep advancing to the net, keep looking for the net, not giving up. Of course, there was a lot of support from the Russian team, from Yevgeny, from Dennis and from my captain. It helps me a lot.

You haven't lost that many matches in Bercy. Are you inspired by this place?

MARAT SAFIN: Yeah, it seems like I cannot lose a match here (smiling). I like that atmosphere. I like the situation of the court. I don't know. I always played well here. I always did a great job. I played my best tennis here in Bercy.

Could you tell us why you had a verbal abuse?

MARAT SAFIN: I had a verbal abuse?

A warning. What did you say?

MARAT SAFIN: I said what I thought about it. I didn't accuse anybody. I didn't want to offend anybody. I just didn't offend anybody. Just I gave my opinion. That's okay. I was pissed because you know also it was a very important ball for me. I was sure the ball was out. But just it happens. I didn't meant anything to the chair umpire at all.

Was it difficult to come back in the match?

MARAT SAFIN: No, it was disappointing because I needed so badly that point. But it's okay. Everybody's making mistakes. I'm not abusing him. I can't do anything. Just it was my opinion.

Now you are on holidays. What are your plans?

MARAT SAFIN: The year's finished. I was waiting for it so badly. I wanted to finish the year with a good match. I played it. Now I can go for vacations being relaxed for a couple of weeks.
