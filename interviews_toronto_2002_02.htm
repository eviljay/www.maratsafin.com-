JULY 31, 2002
Marat Safin - Andrei Pavel (ROM) 6-3, 7-6(7-4).
AN INTERVIEW WITH: MARAT SAFIN

TOUR OFFICIAL: Marat fires 13 aces today. Moves into the round of 16. Will play the Rios-Moya winner for tomorrow.
Questions for Marat.

REPORTER: Hi Marat. First I have to ask you about the tiebreaker. There was a key point in the match and it was 3-3 when one of your serves hit Andre in the face and it seemed to hurt him a little, jar him a bit, and I'm wondering if you can describe that play because he had only one more point after that.

MARAT SAFIN: No. I think it's 3-3. In a tiebreaker you basically need to serve first serve because it's very important and because if you serve to the side you have to serve way wide and, you know, at 3-3 you're a little bit nervous so just serve strong to the body and let's see what happened because it's very difficult to play it and -- I mean -- and I have even opportunities before the point. I had just easy ball, easy passing shot, and I made a lob, and I was making quite stupid mistakes but then -- I mean the serve saved my ass in the tiebreaker. It's different

REPORTER: Marat, you were up about 5-Love in the first set. He then came on. Was it more something he did or was it something you didn't do?

MARAT SAFIN: Well actually nothing. Nothing -- nothing happened. He just -- he broke me but he was -- also I had like five set points and he was serving first and second serve like 220 kilometers per hour. What do you want me to do? It's like good luck and I mean it's -- he played well. He served well. I can't say that I was doing something wrong and he made me break, yeah, of course because I made two double faults but I was playing okay. I was -- I was still under control and when I had the opportunity the second time that I served there -- like it's 5-3 -- I won it easy so it was okay. I wasn't -- I wasn't nervous at that moment.

REPORTER: Marat, were you at all conscious of the fact that you won the tournament here two years ago and he won the tournament in Montreal last year? It was sort of a battle of two champions of the tournament?

MARAT SAFIN: No because it's already history. Two years ago and one year ago, it's already in the past, so you're coming here to win it again and to win the tournament. You don't think that what happened last year and how he played last year. It is just you come here you want to win the tournament. It doesn't matter against who you're playing and just you go for it so it's no. It's a difficult match but not like -- without history.

REPORTER: He played pretty well and he was really fighting pretty hard. Did you sense -- like maybe when you're defending champion you fight a little bit harder to win the title?

MARAT SAFIN: Yeah but also I have to say it's Master series so there are lots of points, lot of money, so you have to fight anyway so but -- what was the question? I forget.

REPORTER: Did he fight like a defending champion?

MARAT SAFIN: Yeah. Yeah. I think -- he played -- I played very good the first set and the second set he was -- he was playing great. He was serving well. He could -- he was playing with a lot of confidence and I was -- I was lucky, you know, that I served well on important points because he was playing -- he was great. He can -- he even start to make second volley and he can play from the baseline. He can play; he can serve well; he can return so he's a very dangerous player and in a tiebreaker it was very, very close and if I don't serve a few times first serve then it's a third set and then you never know.

REPORTER: Marat, the way you're playing now do you feel that your level now is capable of winning this tournament or are you not prepared to look that far ahead yet?

MARAT SAFIN: No, no. I don't -- I don't look so far because I have tough opponent. Rios or Moya . They're both playing well. Moya - he won the tournament like two weeks ago I think -- I think. Yeah. Two in a row. I didn't know. And Rios is always dangerous. He's a lot of talent and I mean he can play a great match and he is always dangerous so just -- let's see what will happen tomorrow. I mean you have to -- you have to play that match and you have to work hard and then, then we can talk about winning a tournament.

REPORTER: Marat, Moya, he won 13 matches in a row. You weren't following tennis? You don't know what's going on?

MARAT SAFIN: I told you I was on vacation so I'm not -- I wasn't at the office so I didn't -- I didn't know any results and just -- I didn't know who was even in the semifinals and the quarter finals in Wimbledon. Yeah.

REPORTER: Good vacation!

MARAT SAFIN: Very good. (Laughter) I need them.

TOUR OFFICIAL: Anything else?

MARAT SAFIN: Thank you very much.
