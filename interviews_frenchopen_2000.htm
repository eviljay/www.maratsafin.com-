After the match in 1/4finals (Safin - Norman) at Roland Garros
Wednesday, 7th of June 2000


Q. In that final game when you missed the first two backhands down the line, was he getting into your head a bit at that point? Were you mentally disturbed by those two errors?

MARAT SAFIN: No, because first of all, I mean, I start 3-Love up in the first set. I start to play not right. Yeah, it was 3-Love, but it wasn't right tactic, it wasn't right game I had to play. It was 3-Love, I made some good shots. But then it was 6-4. Second set I played horrible. First two sets actually I played, I don't know, maybe my worst game. I missed every ball. I couldn't play tennis. Everything was going to the net. Nothing was under control. Of course, these last two balls in the net, it's the same. My head, in the first two sets, I was stupid. I played like really like shit. I'm not afraid of this. That's true. I play like this because I was scared, I didn't know what to do on the court. I had eye like this big (indicating large). I couldn't play tennis. That's it. The last two balls, I make him present for Christmas. That's it.

Q. When you're on the court, you're very open with your expressions. Have you always been like this and does it help you?

MARAT SAFIN: You start to know me very well, huh (laughter)? I'm very sorry for my attitude on the court because I know it wasn't so nice from my part, to the person, to the crowd. It was too much. But I think that they have to understand it. I hope they will understand it. When the things are going in the wrong direction, completely wrong, you have nothing under control, everything you're playing, everything you're doing is completely wrong. You cannot do anything to change it. You get -- I get very pissed at myself. I say to myself, "I cannot be so stupid to play like this." I was sorry, I break three racquets. At least it helps me to win one set, so I think it's not so bad (laughter).

Q. The crowd seemed to enjoy it, actually.

MARAT SAFIN: I hope so. Otherwise, I'm really sorry. I cannot say. Sorry, that's it.

Q. Have you always been this way? Do you remember when you were younger, you were expressive with your emotions?

MARAT SAFIN: Yeah, last year I was very expression, 48 racquets in a year (laughter).

Q. I meant when you were a child.

MARAT SAFIN: I had not a contract, so I couldn't break so many racquets.
Q. How many racquets last year?

MARAT SAFIN: 48, unfortunately.

Q. You were talking about how many racquets today. Was it three, four, five racquets?

MARAT SAFIN: (Indicating three.)

Q. You took the head off of one.

MARAT SAFIN: Of course, if there is some persons, like Enqvist, like Norman, these kind of person, they're very quiet on the court, they can handle it. But there's some other person like me, like Ivanisevic, all the guys who play with prestige, look at them. Arazi, el Aynaoui, me, Ivanisevic, they're completely crazy (laughter). If you ask Goran how many racquets he broke, I think it's almost the same. At least for me, to me it helps a lot. I mean, is not the point to break every match three or four racquets. But today I was really pissed. Sorry, really. I'll not do it again.

Q. After you broke that racquet, after the second set, you seemed to play really well after that for at least a set or so.

MARAT SAFIN: Thank you.

Q. Did you get it out of your system?

MARAT SAFIN: Yeah, it helps me. But first racquet I broke because of the floor. I didn't throw so hard. It just go (indicating). Second, I broke it because I need it. I need it because so many energy was inside of me that, I don't know, I would kill somebody - but it was the racquet. Yeah, I start to play better because I everything was gone and I start to think. But, of course, it was too late. I mean, two sets down. You win a third set. But I didn't serve. I didn't move him at all. I missed easy balls. How you can beat the guy? There's no chance. There's no chance. If I would win today, I think it wasn't right for sure, definitely, because he played much better than me. It was one guy on the court actually. I don't know how he lost the third set. Maybe out of concentration he lost his head a little bit. I was a little bit lucky. But must be very easy, 6-4, 6-3, 6-2. I didn't make anything today. Completely I was just playing -- I didn't know what to do.

Q. How do you explain that you played so bad, as you say, for the first two sets? Is it a mental consequence of all the matches you've been playing for six weeks?

MARAT SAFIN: First of all, I was nervous, of course, because first time in quarterfinals, big chance to get in semis. Unfortunately, you start to think about all these things. You play against No. 1 in the world. He's a great player. You going under pressure all the time. You always put minus, minus, minus, minus, not even one plus. I'm playing good, so at least there's something. i'm no confidence, he's the No. 1 in the world, he won Rome, he made very good results, many things. I mean, like this, of course, you come to the court, I came like loser. Like this you going to play tennis? Too much minus in your head.

Q. What was the main difference in his game from the last time you played him in Barcelona and today?

MARAT SAFIN: No, it was completely same game, completely same style. Everything was the same. But mine, if you -- I talk to Andrei, and we came to the same thing, that I'm not in very, very good shape like it was in Barcelona. I played easy. I didn't even play hard with all my power. But the feelings has gone. Something happened this week that I couldn't -- I was playing, but I couldn't -- I don't know. I didn't play easy like I played there. For me, I came to Barcelona. I played easy. For me, the tennis was - I don't know - I was serving like Ivanisevic in his best times. Serve, backhand, forehand, I do on the court whatever I want. But today, I mean, 200 kilometers from the forehand, two meters out. So many unforced errors. I don't feel comfortable with my game actually.

Q. How annoying was that helicopter hovering over the stadium in the second set?

MARAT SAFIN: Really, if you want to hear, he get me very pissed. Sorry, but when he was going down, going up, even more noise. I don't know from where they came. I hope it was a television, otherwise the guy who rent helicopter, he's completely idiot. I mean, sorry, but we're playing tennis here. I think the crowd, they help me to make him leave.

Q. I guess your expectation now is rest, but what is the next step until Wimbledon?

MARAT SAFIN: First of all, I have to relax a little bit. I need it because I played like one month and a half, every week. I make some good results. Then I go to Queen's. I will try to because I have to. Yeah, I know. If I am professional, I promise to the tournament that I will come, I have to come. It's not the point that I'm sad, I'm upset with myself, I'm playing bad, not to come. If you promise, I think if you shake the hands, you have to come, you have to do it. I think it will be right if I will come. I will try to win one match on grass. I never win one match.

Q. After you play Nottingham?

MARAT SAFIN: Guys, relax.
