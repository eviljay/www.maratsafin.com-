Australian Open - January 21, 2002 Melbourne, Australia
Marat Safin - Pete Sampras 6-2, 6-4, 6-7, 7-6.
An interview with: MARAT SAFIN


Q. Well, Marat, what a match. The first two and a half sets you could do nothing wrong. Did he then pick up his game, or did you let it slip a little bit, or what?

A. No, I just, I made a few mistakes in 4-2. I missed, I missed a very important shot on my backhand because I think I went for too much. I didn't want to hit at Pete because he was on the net, and I miss it. And definitely, it's not the best way to play this point, you know, just to putting the ball down the line. I think you should go for a little bit more. And just this point changed all my match, and could be very dangerous because Pete, he start to play well. He just, he made unbelievable comeback, he started so well, and he was playing good. And now, I'm happy that I'm satisfied also with my game. Even tiebreaker in the fourth set I was down 4-2 and I could finish the match, and I finish the match with a forehand which is very important for me.

Q. Marat, when you missed that forehand on the first match point, what was going through your head?

A. So many things because, first of all, I think you have Pete Sampras at the net and he's so big, so huge, and you just have to make the passing shot, and you have to make it so perfect so that he doesn't get this ball, otherwise you are in trouble. So I was scared, actually, and just, I tried to put the ball, and you cannot. You just, you have to go a little bit for, you have to go for - go for it - just play it, and whatever happens, because against Pete you can't play putting the ball. And that's what I did, basically, in the last match point. I just went for it. It was perfect.

Q. Is that the best you have played since the US Open, today?

A. I think it was a great match. I was, I was tough today. Even, you know, I was a little bit down after the third set. But I stayed there. I fought, and I played, I played great first two, two and a half sets; and even in the fourth set I was there. I played well, and the tiebreak was quite good, a few good returns, and of course it gives you a lot of confidence after this match.

Q. It was a fabulous shot on the final match point?

A. Yeah, it was good. I didn't even expect that I can put the forehand this way, you know, down the line, and having Pete, you know, coming for the net. And I made it. So I was, I was so happy that just, so many, so many things into my head, you know
- "I'm winning, I'm finish, it's already 12.30 at night" - because if you go to the fifth set, you never know how it can finish,. And, of course, it's - it would not be very nice to go five sets with Pete, because you never know. Pete has such a big experience, and you can lose. If you lose this match it's just, can turn your tennis all the way round, from the great match to finish the match horrible and without confidence.

Q. Marat, you had a lot of interaction with the umpire tonight. Were you sort of distracted or annoyed that he talked to you so much?

A. No, I think it's just - I mean, everybody has to understand that. Not only me complaining with the chair umpires. Pete, he had, you know, his points, that he thought the ball was out and the ball was in. So I think it's normal. It's a part of the game. And the chair umpire is also participating in the game. He doesn't have to say, you know, "5-4 for Sampras" and all these things. He has to also be in the match. And, of course, you have some points you have to discuss with him. I think it's normal. I hope nobody is taking it very personal. It's the match, and after the match is a different story. But you just have to understand also the other players who are fighting with the chair umpires, with everybody, because they want, I want to win. You know why I want to win? Because 15,000 reasons inside of the tennis court, and you have to give all your best, I think. That's my point. If somebody just doesn't understand me, it's their problem. I'm trying all my best on the court, all the time I'm going inside, and I'm doing this for the spectators, not for nobody else.

Q. At one stage you changed racquet and Pete seemed to question whether you had sort of, what you were doing. Was there any sort of needle between the players tonight at all?

A. No, I'm great with Pete. On court, I think it's different story. He wants to beat me, I wants to beat him. But just nothing. I change the racquets, and only reason I change racquets is because of the strings. I don't know what was the point, because of the strings. And, of course, he is complaining; but he has to understand also. He is change racquets and nobody complains, so why he should complain when I'm changing the racquets. It's okay. There was no problems with him. I never had a problem and it was great him.

Q. Marat, how do you get - how do you sit with this Rebound Ace compared to the court in the US Open. What is the difference technically? Where do you feel better?

A. Just, it's not such a big difference; but different, different surface. It's - here is, like, I don't know. It's a different material. They bounce more here. The ball bounce more, and - yeah, it's slower, it's a little bit slower than US Open.

Q. It's better or worse for you?

A. It's just different. It's just different. I'm doing well here and I did, I always did well in US Open. So it's just a matter of practice and get used to the surface.

Q. Marat, are you better in your mind than you were last year, and, if so, why?

A. Because everything is organised, you know. Everything was prepared, you know. I prepare myself for this year. I had great preparation. I'm travelling with the people that I want, I'm travelling with my friends, and I'm having fun. It's just, I think it's - I want to have fun and I want to enjoy my tennis, and the only reason I can enjoy my tennis is being with my friends around, and they are helping me and they are giving me the confidence that I need.

Q. Last year was not the case?

A. No. Last year was a little bit different because I got injured. I had problems and I couldn't find my game. I got nervous, and I was just - I was a little bit lost. So I need to stop and I stop for two months, and I just prepared everything for this year. So I'll be dangerous for this year, for sure.

Q. Marat, what about friends? I've never seen so many blondies and groupies in a player box?

A. Definitely.

Q. Are all they friends of yours or - - -

A. They are friends of my friends. Definitely, I think you have to admit that I have unbelievably beautiful bench.

Q. Very good taste.

A. Thank you.

Q. Marat, he's only jealous.

A. It's okay. You can be part of the party.

Q. Okay. Thanks.

Q. Are you living now in Switzerland?

A. Yes.

Q. Too much pressure in Valencia?

A. No, just - I had nothing else in Valencia. It was a great experience. I spent there seven years, and I don't have anything else there.

Q. Not even the girlfriend?

A. No, not any more, not any more. I just moved there. I tried to find something else. I moved to the Geneva and I want to be a little bit more closer to Russia.

Q. Have you been in contact on the phone with Mats this week?

A. No. I have to call him because there's so much - I'm a little lost with the time. I can't even call to my friends in Moscow, because too early, always too late.

Q. Marat, why did you say it was very important for you to finish the match on your forehand?

A. Because it gives you confidence and it's, I think it's - as everybody can see that my backhand is my, is better than my forehand. But also, I get confidence in my forehand, I can be very dangerous. So it's very important for me to finish the match with a great passing shot from the forehand, which will give me so much confidence in the next round that I will need it.

Q. The matter of the running forehand, you hit a bunch of them today?

A. Yeah, I'm trying to improve, you know, even on the match. I'm doing well. I'm happy that everything is working.

Q. And what about the next round?

A. What about the next round: great player, very experienced, and very talented. He made a great come back against Ljubicic. He was losing two sets to Love. He was two sets to Love down and 5-1, and he came back. He won today against Costa, and he has a lot of confidence. But also, he played two matches five sets. So I hope Wayne is not so fresh like he can be. But definitely, it is going to be very tough, very tough.

Q. Were you tired tonight, around the end? Were you ready to play fifth set?

A. Just, for me, it wasn't - because you never know, it's just, you can see the time is, it's too late, and five sets against Pete, and you have to be concentrated against him. You cannot just - and I didn't want to play fifth set. You never want to play fifth set against Pete, because you never know what can happen. He can make such a great return that just, your game is gone, and then he holds his serve and that's it, you are in big trouble. And, of course, I was two sets to Love up, and a break in the third, so then go to find the motivation for the fifth set against Pete.

Q. Do you think he was trying to influence the linesmen at some stage?

A. I don't know. The match is finished. I don't really want to discuss about the linesmen. I had my points and I said to the chair umpire and, yeah, I have, I have too many thoughts about these things. It's okay. I just, I won the match so I can't complain.

Q. Marat, I know that you asked Brojin to stay next to you for these days. What do you want from him? Is it friend just, or a coach, or a future coach, or what? What kind of relationship?

A. No, he's friend. I've known him for a long time. He is a great friend of mine.

Q. Is he helping also technically?

A. Not just that - he is giving, of course he is giving his opinion. It's great, you know, to have the people that, they care about you. And just it's - I'm happy to be next to him, you know. He is staying in my hotel room and we are having fun. It's great. I'm pretty satisfied that he is bringing me luck.

Q. Marat, how many friends do you actually have here, and where are they all from?

Q. The Entourage.

Q. Blondies?

A. It's my team. It's my team and just, enough, enough friends, you know, to support me. I need some support. I need to find the motivation, you know, against Sampras. That's why I brought so many blondes. That's why I have Brojin and all my friends.

Q. Marat, how do you fancy your chances of going all the way here?

A. They are growing. They are growing from day to day. I think I have a big chance to, at least to play well against Wayne. But I don't know how it can finish, but I'm pretty confident, and I'm looking forward to playing against Wayne. And then I will see. Quarter-finals, you really have to take it serious.

Q. With what you saw of Pete tonight, how much longer do you see that he will continue at the top level?

A. I didn't even think about these things, you know. I just, I want to finish the match as soon as possible, and I didn't want to - I saw him for three hours and it's enough for me, you know. It's okay. We can stay friends, but three hours, I think, with Pete, is too much. Believe me, it's way too much. He's too dangerous, too big.

Q. If one day you have to celebrate, will you celebrate first with Brojin or with the blondies?

A. I'll answer you if I win here, okay,
