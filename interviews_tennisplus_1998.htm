June 1998 - From the Russian Magazine "Tennis+"
An interview with Marat and his parents shortly after he came back
from the French Open in 1998.


Question - In the last couple of days you've given lots of interviews. The interest of the journalists is absolutely understandable. But you probably know by now that communication with press has became a part of your job?

Marat - Yes, I was already told this by people from IFT.

Question - These meetings and conversations are in your interest also. When you talk about the match, contestants, about yourself then there is less chance that somebody will be false about how the things really are.

Marat's Father - Unfortunately it has already taken place when they wrote something he didn't tell in the interview.

Marat - I don't really care. Let them write whatever they want.

Question - That's a risky out-giving...Marat, do you realise that journalists misrepresent facts not because they want to offend you or Kafelnikov. Sometimes a reporter catches his ear at something without having a chance to verify the information because the player is not very accessable. That is where improvisations take place. But lets get down to business...You have already given tens of interviews, what haven't you been asked about yet?

Marat - I guess I told everything I ever could about my tennis life. You know, there came Inna Raskazova from "Moskovski Komsomolets" and we had a very detailed conversation. And then she suddenly said: there has already been so much positive said about you, maybe there also is something negative?

Question - What is that your coach in Spain Rafael Mensua gave you that your mother couldn't give?

Marat - Ask any player and he will tell you how difficult it is with your parents. There comes a moment when you simply start sending them to hell in your mind. And if it's a strange person who is working with you, things change. You respect him as a specialist, in the end you pay him money... A success or a failure depend on what relationships you have with your coach. I am friends with mine.

Question - They say about you that you are a formed player of the Spanish school. Do you agree with this formula?

Marat - Well, if they say so... But actually, tell me what innovations in the technique of these or that shots did I get in Spain in comparison to what my mother gave me in Moscow?

Marat's Mother - To call him the forsterling of the Spanish school is the same as to consider Kournikova being the forsterling of the American school. She used to spend the entire summer with us on "Spartak's" courts . Her trips to America can be called practise. But you can be practising now here, tomorrow there, then some place else but the school stays Russian.

Marat - By the way, and what is the core of the Spanish school? We have to decide on what Spanish school is.

Question - In the translation of your match with Courier in the Davis Cup after a couple of obvious mistakes when playing volleys they said: well, what did you expect Spaniards are not too good playing at the net.

Marat's Father - What journalists are saying is just nonsence: "He can't play at the net" What makes them think this way? If Marat is playing really well at the base line, if he doesn't come to the net too often, it is not the basis for such statements. Marat's present game was given to him by his mother - the coach. Rosa liked playing at the net herself. Remember how Marat did at "Roland Garros". Typical episode. I was listening to Alik Mitrivelli's comments: "It would've been great if he approached to the net right now." And Marat as if he could hear him came to the net and successfully won the point...It's just that he is lacking game experience yet. Practice. Not the one that you can get while training but just game practice. By the way, something of that sort happened to Andrei Medvedev, but he just let this moment slip away.

Marat - And all this has nothing to do with the Spanish school. Moya plays really well at the front, comes to the net very often. It shows especially if you are not playing doubles...Alex Corretja plays. Even Alberto Berasategui does.

Question - What about you, are you going to play doubles?

Marat - I can't get anywhere yet, my ranking is too low. But I would like to, with great pleasure. Right now I will try to solve this problem somehow.

Question - Do you have any distinct options?

Marat - I've got a contract with IMG. I asked people from the agency to help me get a wild card for doubles. I want to play. It's interesting, plus the money.

Question - Well, it's not only the money. Kafelnikov explained that he improved his game in singles after he got the feel of the doubles. And money goes next.

Marat - How can I understand this? Money don't go next. Money is always money...Why play doubles? To earn money in the first place. There is nothing to be ashamed of here. Then doubles is the same practice only that you are paid for it. Profitable and pleasant together.

Question - Mixed? Marat - That's not serious.

Question - OK, but lets get back to the question of the "Spanish school".

Marat - Spanish school? Well you will write everything as it is. And what's the good of it? Probably none of the journalists will even read it.

Question - I'm bound to think that they will. We have noticed and not even once that those who write about tennis borrow materials from "Tennis +". In the end we are the only specialised edition.

Marat - I think I have Russian school, and I don't really understand why journalists write about my Spanish school.

Question - Tell now, can it be asserted that the favourite surface is clay?

Marat - I've been playing on clay for 4 years. But I'm doing pretty well on hard too. A couple of weeks of training and everything will come. I don't have like, say Berasategui, such an extreme addiction to clay. For him it really takes a lot of guts to play on hard. But he does play and even not too bad at times. Where did he beat Krajicek? I guess in Paris, in Autumn, on the carpet. And Ferreira - he beat him on hard in America.

Question - Are hard courts widespread in Spain or they only have clay courts?

Marat - There is one satellite on hard. The rest are clay courts and tournaments. Question - Have you played challengers on clay?

Marat - In summer I tried to play in Portugal on hard. Then October through December I played all the tournaments on hard only.

Question - Now everybody quotes Ivan Lendl:"Grass is for cows". What do you think?

Marat - I never even tried to play on grass. Maybe I'll like it.

Question - Let's pass on to something else...Your mother said that you were going to take the exam for drivers licence.

Marat - I've already taken it. Here, take a look.

Question - What car do you have?

Marat - Used "Golf", something like "Felicia". I almost got it for free. It hasn't been a long time since I learned to drive, I don't really want to smash a new car. I'll change it later for "Mercedes". They promised to give it to me already this year.

Question - But to go back to tennis. What are you going to be working at now?

Marat - The main thing is game at the net. The rest if not bad. I just need to refine.

Question - In Moscow, in the Kremlin Cup, you once served at 217 km/h. Marat - This year in Philadelphia at the ATP tournament I served at 221 km/h in the match with Schalken.

Question - What is your standard in general? 195 - 190?

Marat - I don't like American Twists. That's why it goes to 180 and higher.

Question - Is this also something your mother taught you?

Marat - Mother taught me the technique. And then the main things are your motions, catching the rhythm.

Question - When our team was playing Davis Cup finals with Sweden and then with America were you in Moscow or in Spain?

Marat - I was not present for the matches in Olimpiisky.

Question - Could you have possibly thought then that some day you will play in the Davis Cup team for Russia?

Marat - I was not thinking about that.

Marat's Father - I should say here that Marat's been in the Russian team since the age of 13. Marat - I was the 2-3 in the country then.

Question - And who was the first?

Marat - Artiem Derepasko. Question - When did Tarpischev invite you to the Davis Cup team?

Marat - I was in Austria then. Mother said that Shamil Anviarovich Tarpischev called and invited me to play for the team. I first came to the Davis Cup when we were playing with Romania. Our team won. And then Shamil Anviarovich told me about America.

Question - Did he tell you that you were going to be number 2?

Marat - No. Then everybody were in the team: Volkov, Cherkasov, Olhovski. But it happened so that I was the one who played.

Question - Does TV in Spain, in the apartment that you rent, get the channels from Russia?

Marat - No.

Question - So you detached now from life in Russia? You learn about what is going on here from what your parents tell you?

Marat - Yes, in general. You can also learn something from local TV news.

Question - Do you speak English?

Marat - Not too well really. I normally speak with Spaniards and our guys. Well, I can speak English. I don't know if I say properly what I want to express, but here the important thing is to be understood.

Question -...and you haven't had any misunderstandings? You have already said quite a lot of interesting things in Russian.

Marat - What do you mean?

Question - The interview in "Moskovski Komsomolets"

Marat - That's what you mean...the journalist asks me: "Do you drink?" Our conversation took place during the tournament, plus it was the tournament where I was playing for the first time. Was that serious to ask me that? So I answered, in a joke: - I do. - Do you smoke? - I don't. - Did you go some place here? - I did. - What did you drink? - Whisky... That was the funny conversation we had. And then I found out from the newspaper that I liquor up every day. What I just can't understand is how did I manage to have enough strength for seven matches, three of which, with Kuerten, Agassi and Pioline, were five sets matches...

Question - Lets move on. Do you have a girlfriend?

Marat - Her name is Silvia.

Question - Does she also play tennis?

Marat - She used to. She had a trauma of her leg. Her sister still keeps on playing. We are in the same club with her.

Question - You live not far from each other?

Marat - 10 minutes. Valencia is not a big city.
