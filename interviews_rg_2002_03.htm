Saturday, June 1, 2002, Roland Garros
Marat Safin - D. Nalbandian 6-3, 6-3, 3-6, 6-4


Q. How would you rate your performance today?

MARAT SAFIN: The first two sets was great tennis, I think, then I lost my game. I had my match in my hands the fourth set, 3-Love. Just try to make 4-Love. But I just missed everything and I was a breakdown in the fourth. Was a great fight. I'm satisfied the way I fought, but not the way I played in the third and fourth set.

Q. What happened in the beginning of the third when you had the break?

MARAT SAFIN: Just lost my game, you know. Just I start to go back. I start to make rallies with him, which is a mistake. Argentinian players, it's just their game to play on clay, play long rallies, you know, just stay there, hang in there. I start to play his game, and that's why I start to have problems, and that's why I lost my game. My game is just to, you know, make the point shorter, try to look for the net. That's my game. After this, is difficult to find your game when you're already so-so in the first set. Even I had my opportunity 3-Love, and I just -- I didn't play the way I should play.

Q. Is it a problem of concentration?

MARAT SAFIN: No, just like I start to try to play, you know, for sure. I start to play just not to make many mistakes. It's not my game. When I start to play from the baseline, I start to miss anyway. So if I have to look for the net, I have to put pressure, I have to -- I don't know, look for the net all the time. That's my game. And I can't play another game. I'm not good enough, especially if you are playing against Argentinian guy on clay. Is not a good thing to stand back and just to make rallies.

Q. How do you compare your game since the beginning of the tournament?

MARAT SAFIN: Still I make four sets against a guy that he is a great clay court specialist, I think. To make four sets is still okay. I mean, I think it's not so bad. But, you know, could be better. Could finish in three sets. But I was almost in the fifth set against him, so in the fifth set, you never know what's going to happen. These kind of matches you have to finish as soon as possible.

Q. How is your fitness? It was your hip, I think.

MARAT SAFIN: Difficult. I'm too many kilos and I have to run on clay. It's difficult. Believe me, it's quite difficult. But I'm still there, and I have a serve, which is I can save some points, make a few easy points. Otherwise I'm in big trouble.

Q. Now you're in the second week. Do you think it's a new tournament that begins now? Is it something to be in the second week?

MARAT SAFIN: You have to go match by match. Now I'm playing a guy from France. Di Pasquale, he's doing well. He won his matches quite easy, I think. He's confident. He has support of the stadium, so it's difficult to play against. But I think is okay. Second week is already a different game. You have more confidence. Even the match of today is perfect, just to have enough confidence to go to second week. It's new tournament starting. So I think it's good. It's good. I'm feeling comfortable.

Q. You were good friends with Arnaud a few years ago. Do you still have relationship with him?

MARAT SAFIN: Yeah. You know, but the guy had some problems, injury, so he was out on the tour for one year, I think. He didn't play so many matches. I didn't see him for a long time. I know him since 15, 16, so quite good friends. We know each other quite well. We played a lot of matches when we were Juniors. So we know each other very good.

Q. Did you talk with him since the beginning of the tournament in the locker room or players' lounge?

MARAT SAFIN: Yeah, yeah. Quite good relationship.

Q. Back to what you were saying about losing your game, do you almost expect that when you go on the court? Seems like you're going great, then without fail, not always a set, but without fail you can hit a rough spot for a while. Do you almost expect that?

MARAT SAFIN: Can you repeat the question again because I didn't get any word. Try to make it slow.

Q. What you were saying before about losing your game, do you almost expect that when you go on the court, that you're going to hit a rough patch for a little bit? It seems like you play well, then without fail there's always a spot where you sort of get lost, then you come back. Do you almost expect to lose it?

MARAT SAFIN: Of course. I hope I understand your question. I will try to answer your question. In every match, you have this feeling that you are losing your game. It means you are losing a little bit of concentration, you know this. You try to hang in there. But it's difficult. The other guy is feeling this, and he's playing much better because he has more confidence the way the match is going. You have to be careful in this situation because you know, you can feel it, you're losing your game, you're losing your concentration. If you miss this kind of ball, these two kind of balls, probably is the key of the match because you can get in such trouble that you not be able to come over. You have to, I don't know, try to, I don't know, breathe or whatever. Every guy is doing different things. But it's quite difficult. It's quite difficult. Then it starts the choking, which is the worst part of the game. You don't want to get there. You don't want to play these kind of games. So you try to, I don't know, whatever, just walk a little bit, try to concentrate as fast as you can. Because if you lose this moment, you're gone, then you have a problem.

Q. It seems like last year you would hit that patch and never find it. This year we saw it against Rochus even the other day, you'll find your game again. What are you thinking in your head to get your game back?

MARAT SAFIN: I'm getting older, so I know a little bit the game (smiling). I know the game a little bit more. But as soon as you know the game better than before, you start to choke more.

Q. You lose either way.

MARAT SAFIN: No. I give you example. When you're 28, 27, you know the game so well, it's so difficult. When you understand the game very much, you start to choke. You not understand it, but the players understand. It's basically that's the point. So is better to not to understand very well the game, just play your match the way it comes, you know, just hit the shots, just give yourself a little bit of space, just feel free on the court. Because if you start to think what's going to happen after I lose this court, play cross-court, miss it down the line, then you have a problem. Just you need to be a little bit feel free on the court and just play your game. If you miss it, then you have problem. Then you are getting into trouble. But you have to risk sometimes. Sometimes the risk is very important, to be able to risk on some difficult points. What a speech, huh (laughter).

Q. Every time after a victory I ask you if you are happy or not. It's not so obvious even after the match. I would like to know like you have bad news, like you lost your dog or something, or is it because you're very focused and concentrated on the tournament?

MARAT SAFIN: Because I'm not happy?

Q. I always ask you after a victory if you are happy or not because it's not obvious. I would like to know if you have bad news, like you lost your dog last week, or just because you're very focused and serious on the tournament.

MARAT SAFIN: You cannot be very happy when you're winning, you cannot be very sad when you're losing. You have to keep the balance. Is a good answer (smiling)?

Q. You're in balance?

MARAT SAFIN: Yeah, I'm trying to -- not to getting too excited. When you are losing, you can be very sad. I prefer to be okay. When I'm winning, I'm okay. When I'm losing, it's okay.

Q. So it's wisdom?

MARAT SAFIN: Keep the balance.
