Thursday, June 6, 2002, Roland Garros
Marat Safin - S. Grosjean 6-3, 6-2, 6-2

Q. Did you expect it to be so easy?

MARAT SAFIN: It's never easy, especially -- I mean, especially today. But I played quite well. I played smart. And I was focused. I tried to stay calm, tried to play my game. And I was holding during the match. There was a breakpoint in the third set, but I could manage to just to finish the match in three sets, otherwise it could be very difficult.

Q. Is this as well as you would like to play? You would like to play better? You're happy with your game? How do you feel?

MARAT SAFIN: You're never satisfied with your game. You never can be satisfied. You always try to look for better game and try to, you know, serve better, you try to volley better, try to play from the baseline. But I think it's a quite good quality of the game. I think it's good enough to be in the semifinals and to have a chance, you know, to get into the finals. I think if I manage to stay like this - focused, and try to play my game - I think I have a chance.

Q. How about compared to how you felt Saturday in Dusseldorf when your back was hurting you? You must be pretty pleased.

MARAT SAFIN: It was a difficult week for me because I lost to Kiefer, I couldn't even finish the match against Acasuso. I was playing without any confidence. I was quite lost in my game. But then suddenly I came here and I start to play each day better and better. I was feeling great in the court yesterday. And today I was quite -- no, very good. Even me, I was impressed with my game (smiling).

Q. How does it feel to be here with three Spanish players now?

MARAT SAFIN: No, just you have to give also opportunity to other players, not just from Spain. For me, it's an honor to be in the semifinals and to have -- to still to fight to win this tournament. It's going to be difficult. The Spanish, they are always tough on clay. They are playing well. But I think I'm good enough and I think I have enough game to at least to try to beat them. And I think -- I'm looking forward. I would love to try myself tomorrow, try to make it into the finals. Why not?

Q. Because of your own story, you have special connections with Spain. Does it make it special?

MARAT SAFIN: "Special"? Maybe, I don't know. I don't know if it's special or not. But I know them quite well. I know the way they play. They are the biggest fighters in the world. They have very good baseline, very focused. They never miss a shot. But the match where you have to look for a game and try to put under pressure - I mean, in this case Ferrero - and try to serve well. You definitely need a good serve, try to look for the net, try to stay with him, try to stay calm.

Q. So you think the serve will be one of the keys of the match against Ferrero?

MARAT SAFIN: I think that's enough, you know, just to serve well, you know, try to do something on his serve, like not to play his game, because his game is quite good. He plays very well from the baseline. You need to do something else. You need to go to the net, try to do something. You need to do something against these kind of players.

Q. How would you describe all those years you spent in Spain? What kind of memories do you have?

MARAT SAFIN: The best memories, I think, thanks to -- thanks to Spain, I'm here. Thanks to my first coach, that he was with me since I was 14 up to 2000. I mean, they gave me everything. I learn how to play on clay. It's where I took my first points, ATP points. I feel great, you know, that I have such a great school, like Spanish school. I think it's the most difficult one. You have to be really tough to be able to pass it. And they treat me very well. All the Spanish players, they've been treating me very well, they've been nice to me. If I will try to find something bad about Spanish, I mean, it's difficult. They treat me well. I can't say nothing about them. But also I know them very well, the way they play, the way they think. And I think it's good for me - I mean, for the match of tomorrow.

Q. Is there any part of your personality that is "Spanish" in a way?

MARAT SAFIN: The way I'm playing on clay?

Q. No, your personality.

MARAT SAFIN: I don't know. I grew up in Spain. Maybe I have some things from Spain. I think it's not bad. I think it's good.

Q. Do you still have contacts with Rafael?

MARAT SAFIN: Yes, I have. And he's doing well. He opened academy. He's doing well. He has a big family. He has to take care of the kids, try to make some money, and live his relaxed life. I'm really proud of him. I have to say thanks to him. I mean, every day I have to say thanks to him because he was the only one who actually believed in me. And it was an extraordinary man for me. And I think it's great. It was a great six years together. We are still friends. Also he became more than a coach. He became actually my second brother or maybe father. He knew me very good. I mean, great relationship.

Q. A couple things, you didn't play too well in the semifinal in Australia against Haas at the beginning of the match. You didn't play too well against Johansson at the end of the match. Did you learn things from that now that you're down to the final rounds here?

MARAT SAFIN: No, because it was different story. Against Johansson, it's difficult. Not many people, they understand that Johansson is very dangerous player, very dangerous player. He won Montreal I think two, three years ago. He's very solid tennis player. He has a great serve that you can't even read it, great return, great baseline. You know, he can play from the volley. This day, he was just better than me. I was maybe a little bit under pressure, was a little bit nervous, I couldn't find my game. But that's it. But still, it was four sets. He won 7-6 in the fourth. Okay, I didn't play probably my best game. But this day he was better. You cannot judge me. I mean, I tried. I tried hard. You cannot not try in the finals, especially if you are there, you have to try. And I did it, but just I wasn't good enough this day. But I think it's a different story. I learned from this a lot. And I think it's -- I hope it's going to work this time - if I will get there.

Q. You beat Ferrero three times in very important matches in the past. Do you think it can give you an advantage?

MARAT SAFIN: I think so. I think so. I know him also since we are 13 basically, playing together at European championships. I know his game, he knows my game. I always play well against him. I like his game. I feel comfortable against him.
