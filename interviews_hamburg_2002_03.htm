19.05.2002
Tennis Masters Series Hamburg
Press conference with MARAT SAFIN

Federer vs. Safin 6:1, 6:3, 6:4

Question (Q): You played him twice before. What was different today? You didn’t get started so well. What would you say?

Safin: I didn’t play very well today as you can see in the score 6:1, 6:3, 6:4. It was quite easy. I couldn’t bring my tennis to the court. I was a little bit upset. I just couldn’t, I didn’t play well.

Q: In the third set you were coming back.

Safin: Yes, I was a break up. But still I was looking, I was fighting. I couldn’t do much playing against him. He played too good today. I couldn’t do much in the match.

Q: Your first serve also didn’t go well I guess. That’s a big disadvantage for your game.

Safin: Yes, but I couldn’t. I didn’t feel really good on the court today. My serve didn’t work. He was just calm and he played the best match of his life. I could feel it, the serve, the forehand and everything, he was pushing. He was full of confidence. He was too confident today.

Q: Is there a special reason why it didn’t work today? Especially in the warm-up, did you feel bad?

Safin: No. I think he liked my game. I was missing too much from the beginning. I was not confident enough. I didn’t start really well. Too many mistakes, too many unforced errors. That’s how it was. And then he took advantage of my game.

Q: When did you notice during the match that you would possibly lose today? Was there a special moment? I thought in the second set I noticed resignation in your game?

Safin: No, the way it started. I could see it was going to be difficult. And then it was 2: 0 I just understood that’s it. I was fighting when the break happened in the third set, it was really tough to play in three sets. I tried to win in five. I tried, I thought maybe if I win that third set and if I can make a break in the fourth set that maybe we could talk about something. But it didn’t work. And when he made the rebreak in the third set. I knew it was the end of the story.

Q: I guess you went into to match with a special tactic? Could you follow that tactic at all?

Safin: I said I couldn’t. I tried to play close to the baseline, I tried to play fast. But I didn’t feel that I had my timing there. I didn’t feel very good. It was a bit too late all the time, like 2 seconds. And he was dominating. My game is to dominate another opponent. It is sad. It is a final of super nine and I was playing great against Hewitt. Yesterday I played quite well against Robredo. And I couldn’t play my game against him. That’s is a pitty. I think I was the favorite, because I have more experience than him. But I couldn’t do much today.

Q: You moved to Spain when you were very young. So you are used to the heavy red clay. But your biggest success was on hard court. How would you rate your favorite court? Do you consider yourself like a hard court player?

Safin: When I moved to Spain I was 14 and I was 1.60 meter. I was made on clay. But now I’m 1.95 meter. Now it is difficult to move on clay for me. Hard court is an easy game. It is more simple. You serve and do a return. It is quite fast. Here you have to work and move. For us tall guys it is very difficult. It is not that I’m lazy. You have to understand I’m too tall for clay. I can make good results, of course I can play on clay. But it is more difficult than it is on hard court.

Q: You have lost twice in Hamburg. Will you come back and try again?

Safin: Yes. Every year, every time, I’ll try. It is a big tournament. I wish I come here one time and win. It is a good preparation for the French Open. It is really a difficult tournament. If you can manage to win this tournament you are a big player.

Q: You suggested two years ago that you might have been too nervous to win against Gustavo. What happened this time. Were you too nervous or?

Safin: No, I had no opportunity to be nervous it was so fast. You need to have time to get to this point. I’m not nervous, I’m just pissed with myself. I couldn’t do anything. It ridiculous that I am no. 1 in the world and I couldn’t do much against Federer. I lost quite easy in 3 sets. That’s the point. It is sad but it is true.

Q: Next week you will play World-Team-Cup with Yevgeny Kafelnikov. Do you help each other too for the French Open?

Safin: Yes. It is a good tournament for the preparation. You play one day and have the other day off. It is a good preparation. You play singles and doubles and have a bit of fun that’s it basically. It is a tough one. The last two years we made it to the finals and had a big chance to win it we were the favorits. Hopefully we can win it this year. We are looking forward to be the champions this year.

Q: Do you have a problem that the next tournament will be without Marc, because he plays the French qualis? Is there a problem for you?

Safin: No. First you have to understand that he is not my coach. He is just trying to bring something in. He wants to try. But this doesn`t mean that in the future he is going to be my coach. We’ll see after the French Open. Of course he has the right to go to the French qualis and maybe play the main draw of Wimbledon. First of all he has to settle is tennis career. He has to settle is life. And then we can talk about business. He has to settle his career first.

Q: What are you taking away from this week? Losing the final or becoming no. 1?

Safin: Both things. It is good to be no. 1 in the race. Of course it is good a match to think about my tennis what I need for the future. I think it is experience. Every day you learn something new. I learned a lot of things today.
