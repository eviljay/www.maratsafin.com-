Safin takes President's Cup singles title

17th of September 2000 TASHKENT, Uzbekistan
Russian rising star and U.S. Open champion Marat Safin won his fifth title of the year Sunday with a smooth 6-3, 6-4 defeat of Italy's Davide Sanguinetti in the President's Cup ATP tournament.
The 20-year-old Safin took the singles title in the $500,000 tournament in just one hour and two minutes - then prepared to head for the Olympics in Sydney, where he is ranked No. 1 on the Russian team.
Safin, seeded second, dominated the President's Cup, winning every match. The towering youth came to the Uzbekistan tournament fresh from becoming the first Russian to win the U.S. Open, with a victory last week over American tennis giant Pete Sampras.
"I will ask my opponent's forgiveness for playing so well," Safin, grinning sheepishly, said after Sunday's match.
The play started even, but then Safin, playing strongly at the net, took the lead in the fifth game of the first set and held it.
The win gave Safin his sixth career title, and fifth of this year alone. In April, he emerged from a slump that almost prompted him to quit the sport, winning in Barcelona, Mallorca and Toronto before the U.S. Open victory in New York.

Super Safin - Russian wins US Open title

10th of September 2000 NEW-YORK, USA (AP)
Marat Safin is the US Open champion! In a one-sided final, he defeated four-time champion Pete Sampras 6-4, 6-3, 6-2 in 98 minutes. It is the first Grand Slam title for the sixth seeded Russian.
"I never played better tennis than today," a happy Safin said after the final. "I was very nervous in the last game, but in the end I was lucky."
"I give him all the credit in the world," a gracious Sampras commented. "He played so well. He had an answer to everything I tried."
Safin was the fresher player and clearly dominated the complete match. He put Sampras constantly under pressure from the baseline, made only 12 unforced errors, returned extremely well and played a couple of impressive backhand passing shots. Sampras, who was only broken four times in the whole tournament up to the final, lost his serve four times during the match. By contrast, Sampras himself had no break points at all up to the very last game, when Safin was obviously nervous.
Both players held serve up to three all in the first set, when Safin broke Sampras. After 31 minutes, he took the first set 6-4. In the second set, it was the seventh game again that Safin broke Sampras. Leading 5-3 on Sampras's serve, the Russian played a brilliant topspin lob to earn a set point. Sampras double faulted to give Safin the set 6-3.
Five times throughout his career, the American managed to come back from being down 0-2, but Safin was just too strong. He continued his impressive performance, hitting aces, passing shots and deep retuns. Towards the end of the set, the Russian became a bit nervous and allowed Sampras two break points, he kept cool and converted his first match point in appropriate style with a backhand passing shot.

Safin lifts the trophy - Safin wins Masters Series Toronto

6th of August 2000 TORONTO, Canada (AP)
Marat Safin is the new Tennis Masters Series Canada champion after a blistering 6-2, 6-3 win over Harel Levy in the final. The win marks the first ever Tennis Masters Series title for the twenty-year-old Russian.
But although Safin was simply too big and too strong today, Levy, who came through qualifying, can still look back on a career best week. He won seven matches before coming up against Safin and though he was overpowered he was far from disgraced.
Indeed it was the Isreali who started more brightly, showing very few nerves in the biggest occasion of his tennis career. He broke Safin in the Russian's first serve game but despite his serve malfunctioning, Safin's mammoth ground strokes were finding their mark with increasing regularity. He broke straight back and demolished the Levy serve a further two times on his way to taking the opening set.
The pattern continued in the second set, with the Russian dominating early on and never relinquishing his grip on the match, despite Levy hanging in for all he was worth.
After the match, a delighted Safin warmly congratulated Levy on his performance and then charmed the crowd with a gracious speech, thanking them for their support during what has been the most successful week of his career.
The Russian will move to number 4 in the ATP Champions Race when the standings are released on Monday. While Levy can temper his disappointment with the fact that his performance here has already earned him direct entry into the Tennis Masters Series Cincinnati draw next week.

Fighting back - Safin rallies to defeat Pavel in Stuttgart

19th of July 2000 STUTTGART, Germany (AP)
Third-seededMarat Safin of Russia fought from behind to beat Andrei Pavel of Romania in three sets Wednesday and reached the second round of the $1 million ATP Mercedes Cup tennis tournament.
Safinwon 5-7, 6-3, 6-3.
Up a break and leading 5-2, Safin lost the next five games to drop the first set, although he had a chance to serve it out at 5-3. But some loose serving that produced a string of double-faults left the Russian a set down.
Safin quickly rebounded, however, and cruised through the next two to advance. The Russian has had a strong season on clay, after a slow start to the year. He won back-to-back titles in Barcelona and Mallorca, then played in the final of the Masters Series in Hamburg and reached the quarterfinals of the French Open.
