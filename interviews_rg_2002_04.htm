Monday, June 3, 2002, Roland Garros
Marat Safin - A. Di Pasquale 3-6, 6-4, 6-3, 6-2

Q. It seems that the first set was not going your way. What was the problem?

MARAT SAFIN: The problem, it was like, first of all, I wasn't -- he start to play very well, he start to push from the forehand, he played very fast. I was 3-2 up I think with a break, right? Then just I lost a little bit of concentration and he played really well. Then second set, I just found my game. More or less I start to do what I had to do from the beginning, just doesn't let him to dominate the game.

Q. Were you surprised that he played so consistently in the first set?

MARAT SAFIN: No and yes. I mean, first of all, because he's not playing for a long time. He was injured. And I wasn't surprised because he won three matches here, was playing great. But is difficult, you know. After a long time, didn't play many matches, so it was really impressive the way he start to play the match. It was very dangerous. I was a little bit scared even in the beginning. I thought that he going to play like this for -- if he can play for a long time...But as you can see, if I can find my game, then it's different story.

Q. What do you think about your game today - especially about your serve?

MARAT SAFIN: I think my serve saved my game today. I think was very important to hold your serve and try to play your game, even if it didn't work at the beginning. I mean, I was nervous. He played well. But then I just start to do what I had to do, you know, just push him, make him run a little bit. And you can't let him play from the place, because from the place he can play really good. He has a very good topspin, he is very fast, he is very good and patient. Is dangerous. I could manage after losing the first set, I could manage to find it. And I'm satisfied the way I played, the way I served, and the way I fought.

Q. Physically you look very good.

MARAT SAFIN: Yeah, quite well. I played a few tough matches. But physically, I'm really good. My masseur is doing quite a very good job. I'm satisfied. I'm happy to be in the second week and to be in the quarterfinals, to play against Grosjean. I'm looking forward.

Q. So Grosjean.

MARAT SAFIN: Tough match. French player. Public against me. The same story like today. It's very important to start well against him because he's playing quite well. He has a lot of confidence now. Central court. It's going to depend on me, the way I will start. If I will start early and make an early break, then it's going to be -- then I can dominate the match. If I will start a little bit lazy, he will make me a break or he will win the first set, I will be in trouble. So it depends on me.

Q. Do you feel as well in this tournament as you felt in the Australian Open, for example?

MARAT SAFIN: Like what?

Q. Your game.

MARAT SAFIN: No, it's different story. I mean, hard court is a hard court. Here clay. Clay change a lot. Still if you are losing, I don't know, 5-3 or even 5-Love, you can change the game. So you have more options here. But you have to be more concentrated here because if you lose your game on clay, it's difficult. You can come back but, you know, like especially tomorrow or day after tomorrow, it's difficult to come back against a French player. You have to be careful. But, still, it has nothing to do with Australian Open. It's a new Grand Slam - different one - and you have to be careful with every player.

Q. Marc Rosset was in the semis here. Did he bring you some good advice about clay and Roland Garros?

MARAT SAFIN: Yeah, but I think I have more experience than him on clay (smiling). I basically have been born on clay. I played a lot of satellites on clay. Still I make quarterfinals in 2000. I have a tough match against Norman. I can also play here and I can make some good results, I can be dangerous. But, of course, he told me few times that he made the semifinals here. He could have -- I know even why he lost in the semifinals here. It was a secret, so (laughter). But it was okay. It was a good experience. I think it's a pity for him that he was in semifinals, play Kafelnikov in the finals, he would never lose against him. Kafelnikov and him, they have the discussion, who would win the finals here in '96.

Q. A question about the crowd. They weren't necessarily on your side today. You seemed to use that to your advantage. You enjoy going out there and entertaining the crowd.

MARAT SAFIN: I try, but they were like, you know, sometimes in the third or fourth set, they were completely against me. So you're feeling a little bit lonely on the court. But, I mean, you have to understand I'm playing in France, I'm not playing in Russia or other country. So it's okay. I think you can survive like this. Still you can win some matches.

Q. What were you doing? One point in the seventh game of the third set where you started jumping up and down. Were you trying to get the ballboy's attention?

MARAT SAFIN: No, because I tried to get the ball from the ballboy. But the ballboy, he was too busy, with each other, I think (smiling). I was trying to repeat the mistake, just to hit the ball again. But the ballboy, he didn't care about me anymore.

Q. In 2000, when you arrived in the quarterfinals, you were mentally very tired. Is it totally different now?

MARAT SAFIN: It's two years later. I mean, I played a lot of matches there. I have no such experience like I having now. I was just climbing up, No. 12 in the world. I didn't have so much experience like I'm having now. I consider myself a little bit more clever than two years ago. I know what I have to do. Even now I can make mistakes, but still I would not make mistake that I did in 2000. So it's a little bit different story.
