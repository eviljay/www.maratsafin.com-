After the match in 1/4finals (M. Safin -P. Sampras)
at Tennis Master Series in Toronto
Friday, 4th of August 2000
The biggest win of his career




TOUR OFFICIAL: Okay. Just to let you know, this is Marat's third career semifinal at a Masters Series event. He reached the Hamburg semifinals and then went on to the finals in Hamburg last year and also in -- this year. And in Paris Indoor, '99, went on to the finals there as well. First question for Marat? _

REPORTER: Marat, you hit a fabulous backhand cross-court passing shot at 8-7, the last match point he had. You must have been very pleased with that, when you took your time and there was a lot of pressure on you, you hit it very well.

M. SAFIN: But also I had a chance to make it on a -- on my match point at 6-5. It was like 15-40, and I had like normal passing shot, but too many things I had in my head, I had Pete Sampras, I had central court, I had match points, I had too many things in my head to make it. So I missed it. But eventually I make a few good ones which they helped me a lot. But I'm satisfied with this. If I would make this passing shot in the match point, I think it would be unbelievable to finish this match like this.

REPORTER: It wouldn't be so exciting if you won at 6-5 in the third set, the tie-breaker was good.

M. SAFIN: Tie-break? For you maybe it was good, but for me I lost ten years of my life. But also I had just -- I just told myself that I can do it, that I have enough head, enough balls to beat him, which is very important to me. That not so easy to beat, I can play also a tie-breaker against Pete which is always, who, you know, in a tie-breaker make a few good serve, a few good returns, that's it, the tie-breaker gone. So you're always under pressure. Just, I was lucky, I was patient and I made it.

REPORTER: Is this the best win of your career?

M. SAFIN: Yes, I think so, because he was number one in the world. He's the best player of -- ever we have. There wasn't another player better than him. I think it's very important for me. Also to beat him in a tough match. Because if you beat him 6-2, 6-2, everybody knows that he didn't play his best tennis. But if you play him 7-6 in the third, of course everybody knows that he wants to win. So I'm satisfied also with this, it wasn't 6-2, 6-2, so easy.

REPORTER: Why did it take you so long to come to the press conference?

M. SAFIN: I have to take care of myself, also. Sorry. I have to make some massage, smell good, shower.

REPORTER: Yesterday when I talked to you, you said that the last time that you played Sampras, that you were afraid to beat him. What changed for this match?

M. SAFIN: No, just because Alexander Volkov told me he beat him twice, so I said, "Why can't I beat him?" I mean, if he beat him twice, there's nothing -- I don't have to be afraid. Also, I'm not losing too much. I just have to play, I have to be a hundred per cent sure what I want in the match. You cannot think match when you make a return or make a passing shot, you just have to be sure. Okay, pass it down the line, you pass it down the line. You don't have to think too much about it. Which was a mistake on the match point, when I missed my cross-court passing shot. You don't have to do this against him. You just have to be concentrated and sure in your mind what you have to do.

TOUR OFFICIAL: Just to clarify, Alexander Volkov that Marat just mentioned, was a former tour player. He's the tournament director of the Kremlin Cup, in Moscow, and is travelling with Marat this week and has travelled with him a few weeks.

M. SAFIN: Just like friends.

TOUR OFFICIAL: And his friend.

REPORTER: You've switched several coaches. Can you explain why you've done that and how it's helped you?

M. SAFIN: No, I think I didn't change them so often, just I quit with my old one that I had for six years. It just was too many years. We didn't respect each other like coach and player, he was more like my father. It was a little bit different. It was a little bit tough. Same like you travel with your father, the same feeling. You start once where you start to be nervous, everything is not good atmosphere to be in the top, just like a family, small family. So I put Andre -- we had a small misunderstanding, a very simple one, nothing wrong with -- nothing from other world, just one small misunderstanding. Also, he couldn't travel a lot with me because he has two kids, wife, many things to do and also Tony Picard, it was only in the grass court tournaments. After this, I don't have anybody. So it was just two simple things. Andre, he couldn't work with me because he had other things to do. And Tony Picard can't travel because he was a lot of years on the tour. He could not do it. So now I'm free. Nobody loves me. Nobody wants to work with me. And I'm just travelling with my friend, because he's making big favour. I'm really appreciate this that he's doing for me these things at U.S. Open, but he also has a job, a business in Russia, he's a tournament director, so I cannot ask for more. So I will find a solution after U.S. Open. We'll see.

REPORTER: Marat, I know in the past, you've broken a few racquets over the course of a tournament. And I'm wondering, Pete mentioned the fact that you were mentally strong today during the match. And I wondered if it was hard for you to contain your emotions?

M. SAFIN: No, actually I broke too many this year. I broke too many last year. And today also I have the same feeling when he had a match point, it was like 8-7, when he served he made double fault. Before match point I was thinking about how to break the racquet. Suddenly he make the double fault. So I was lucky there. So, I mean, it's not a question to break so many racquets, but sometimes you need this and I think the people, they understand this because we are human beings and we need to try to break it because it's only a racquet, it's nothing else. But I know it's not very nice on the court. It doesn't look very, I would say, very educated. But, sorry, we are players. A lot of things happens in the court, in your head, all these difficult matches, 7-6 in the third. So it's many things. So sometimes you just break it and you don't think about what will happen after. You are just thinking about the racquet, then you need it. You can't live without it.

REPORTER: Marat, I hope this doesn't sound like a silly question, but if you were a tennis fan, coming tomorrow to watch the semifinals, would you be excited about the matchups?

M. SAFIN: What do you want me to answer? You don't have to ask me, you have to ask the people. If you want to take the microphone, you can go to the crowd and ask them, "Are you going to come tomorrow, or no?" How can I answer this question? I think it's not a silly question, because I don't know the answer. I don't know. There's no Agassi, there's no Sampras. There's no Kafelnikov. There's no -- I don't know, Rafter in the semifinal. It's a little bit -- Novak, Levy, and Safin and Ferreira. You know the answer. I don't think so.

REPORTER: You had quite a few fans calling out your name during the match and you seemed to have quite a large gallery of supporters out there, and I wonder if you could comment on whether that surprised you and how you felt about it?

M. SAFIN: I think I can't believe that there is more Russians than Americans here. It's unbelievable. It's everywhere, even in locker rooms, there's Russians. I mean, I never saw so many Russians in a tournament. And everybody wants to help you and everybody knows about tennis, a lot. I mean it's nice, but sometimes you know, "You want to play for me, or what?" You know, they come to you with, "Come on, Marat, this point, you have to hit the forehand down the line, you have to serve cross-court." I mean, you get surprised because the people they understand so much about tennis. Actually sometimes they say the right things, but unfortunately they are not tennis players, otherwise there would be a lot of genius on the court.

REPORTER: Just about playing Wayne Ferreira tomorrow, how do you feel about that?

M. SAFIN: He was a top 10. He won here and also he surprised a lot with his big return. So he can play from the baseline. He is a good volleyer. It will be tough, but I beat him once, one year ago, a long time ago, but it will be different and it's just a question of to be patient and just to do your things. You can't expect to beat him very easily because a semifinal. He's also got a lot of confidence. He beat Krajicek, Kafelnikov. I don't know who he beat before, but it means that he's in good conditions. So I will just have to play like today, I played a hundred per cent. I just was -- I knew what I need to do, I was concentrated. I think if I play like this, playing right tactically, you can do it. I mean you can't expect very much, but I hope if I will do well my job, I will come, I will play right. I think I can beat him.

TOUR OFFICIAL: Okay. So that's everybody. Thanks a lot.

M. SAFIN: Thank you
