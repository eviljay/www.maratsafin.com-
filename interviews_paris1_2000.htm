November 17, 2000
M. SAFIN - Alex Corretja 7-6(5), 6-3
An interview with: MARAT SAFIN


Marat Safin eliminated Alex Corretja 7-6(5), 6-3 on Friday to advance to the semifinals in Paris.


MODERATOR: Questions, please.

Q. Finally, Grosjean was tougher than Corretja for you.

MARAT SAFIN: It was little bit different, because yesterday I had to fight against all the stadium. Today, at least I had some people with me, some supporters. It was a different game, because yesterday was fast. It was fast game. Here, it was long points, big bounces. It was a question of, you know, fight for every ball and trying to put him under pressure. Sebastien is playing very fast, so you have to be pay more attention and fast balls. And he will have to look, and just be patient. Because Alex is very fast, and he is playing very lot of top spin. So the ball is going up, and if you don't hit the ball well, it's a little bit difficult. But I was patient. I think I make a good play. And I am satisfied.

Q. Were you nervous, considering the closeness of the Champions Race?

MARAT SAFIN: I think just it's a good experience. I think I spend three years - three years on ATP Tour, is enough that I be nervous in these kind of matches. You know, when you fight for something. In this case, I am fighting for No. 1 with Guga. Still, I'm making great, great tennis. I think both of us, we are with lot of confidence and we are playing, I mean, spectacular game. He played unbelievable well against Patrick. Serve and volley, he was passing him everywhere. And me, I'm trying to, you know, be solid on the court, just not to show my emotions too much, because is not helping me a lot. So I'm trying to be patient, confidence, be cool on the court, and not get mad with anybody, not with the ball boys, and all these things. This is a big chance for me to become No. 1.

Q. Your biggest crisis out there was facing first the three set points and then another on your own serve at 4-5. How did you feel at that point?

MARAT SAFIN: I get - I was very, very nervous, and I was very angry with myself because, you know, you start to play well. You make a break in the beginning for the set, and serve didn't work at all. I think it was -- the percentage was very low. So it's difficult to play like this against Alex. He will fight for every ball. He will run. He will hit it with the strings, everything is going to be inside. I couldn't understand what is happening, because I'm not playing bad. But I was making, you know, I was going too fast, I think. Then I had imagine - he had sets points. First of all, he was serving for the set. He make some stupid mistakes, I mean, he knows it. And also Love-40, I just played very well. Then I just, I start to - I said to myself, "You can't play like this because you are too nervous. You have to be a little bit cool and just play your game. You don't have to change your game because it's Alex Corretja." I think I played my game. Then the second set, because was quite easy. Would be much more easy, but that's life. (smiling)

Q. If you ever go to the final with Guga, what do you think will be your greatest difficulty in playing against him?

MARAT SAFIN: We played already twice this year, twice in the final, twice 7-6, and a fifth and a third set. In Hamburg, was 6-5 for me, love-30. I made big mistake to wait for his mistakes. Tiebreak, 7-6 in the third, also it was - it is a lottery. You can't do anything there. It's lottery. If you are lucky, two balls, you win the tiebreak. If you have not lucky, if you miss one millimeter, you lose the tiebreak. That was, I think, the big mistake. I didn't push him enough in the first sets. I didn't push him when I had to push. So I have to change something. I have to push him since the first ball until the last ball. No, don't let him to get to his game, because when he's pushing, he's gonna play unbelievable tennis. But when you are pushing him, is at least you have a chance.

Q. Next one could be Santoro. What are your thoughts about that?

MARAT SAFIN: Thank you for telling me. (smiling) Thank you. What do you want to tell me? What do I need to tell you?

Q. You beat him last time. What did you get from that?

MARAT SAFIN: First they have to play. After, I will see. But I think is gonna be different game. I beat him at least once is more than enough for me in my life. I don't need anything more. I am happiest man in the world. I was happiest man in Stuttgart, but only for one day. And I think it's - as far as the crowd, if he wins, the crowd is gonna be with him. He is last French guy that is in draw. Semifinal. So it's gonna be tough. But I know really well how to play against him. He knows really well how to play against me. I think is different game. It's gonna be different already. Different story.

Q. When you talk about Guga and your matches with Guga, you said Hamburg, which is clay court, Indianapolis which is hardcourt. Here is indoor. Guga has never won an indoor tournament. Are you surprised he's never won an indoor tournament until now, and do you think this is the weakest surface for him?

MARAT SAFIN: I think is not his best surface because - in Brazil, there is no indoor courts. Very simple. He was born on clay. He can play on outdoor. Indoor is not normal in Brazil, I'm sure. But for sure, he gonna win. For sure, he gonna win indoor tournament because he has enough game, he has a huge serve, good ground strokes, very fast hands. The thing, I hope - I hope that that will not be here. Not because I don't like him. Because you understand we are fighting for No. 1, and it will be my mistake if I will let him win this tournament. But I think he gonna win. Definitely, he gonna win few tournament, because he has everything. But I'll be there to not let him. At least this year. Next year, if he wants, I will let him win. (laughing)

Q. Can you compare the pressure of playing a US Open final and the pressure of fighting for the No. 1 spot?

MARAT SAFIN: You can't compare final of US Open with Pete Sampras and, I don't know, final with somebody from No. 1. I felt only one thing, playing final of US Open with Pete Sampras. I don't know how I'm gonna feel in the final of fighting for No. 1 here. I don't know. For a moment I am not in the final. If I am going to be there, you can ask me, and I will tell you what is the worst thing.

Q. You seem to take your time to win each point. Before, you used to end the point very quickly. Is that what you learned with Andrei Chesnokov?

MARAT SAFIN: Not only with Andrei Chesnokov, with Alexander Volkov. You remember how he played. He never moves at all. He never bend his knees in his life. It was the easiest person in the world. But unbelievable talent, and he knew - he knows very well tennis. He can see from other side, which nobody can understand this side. But for him, tennis is too simple. I don't know, for him is not fun. It's too simple. When it's too simple, it's not interesting. I think it was his big problem when he was a player. But of course, Andrei, he helps me a lot. But also, you know, two sides. You know, the side of Andrei Chesnokov was a big factor. The side of Alexander Volkov was a huge thing. Both things, you start to learn something. If you don't learn from these two people then you are...(knocking on wood)... stupid. (laughter) It would be stupid not to learn anything from these guys. I mean, both of them, they were greatest players in Russia, one of the best players in the world. And with all my respects, I mean, these guys know very well tennis. And even if they didn't - if Sasha, he never was in the Top 10, but he had the potential. But he knows very well more than some guys from the Top 10 right now.

Q. What special things did you learn from your Spanish coach (Rafael Mensua)? Actually working with the game now, was there a lot of wisdom that you maybe got from him?

MARAT SAFIN: Just because the question I think - the problem was at the beginning of the year that I didn't - we didn't understand each other. We didn't listen each other. I needed somebody at the time, you know, in the spring, I need someone who can explain to me the guy with a lot of, you know, well respect, you know, who knows tennis.So I will need to listen another guy, because I couldn't - I couldn't realize, I couldn't understand what the coach wanted to tell me. Then, Andrei Chesnokov came, and he explain to me in very simple words with, you know, that let you know that you are a little bit like this (knocking on wood). "You have to do this, this, this, this." Few things that you need to do to be good player. I start to do this. I change my - little bit my confidence changed. My mental head is working a little bit more. So now is little bit - is much more easier to work again with my old coach, because we start to understand each other a little bit better. But even now we fight, but is okay, because I love him.

Q. The race for No. 1 is very, very tough. It could be decided in Masters Cup in Lisbon. Pete Sampras hasn't played for quite some time. Do you think he has a good chance to become No. 1?

MARAT SAFIN: Pete Sampras?

Q. What do you think his chances in Lisbon are?

MARAT SAFIN: His chances?

Q. His chances in Lisbon.

MARAT SAFIN: I think he doesn't need to practice here. I mean, he doesn't need to play so many tournaments. His tennis is very simple: Serve, volley, sometimes forehand. That's it. But for him, it's simple. Doesn't - if he wants, if he propose to himself that he wants to win something, I think he can do this. But also, there is another guys like me, like Guga, like Magnus, like Kafelnikov who also want to win also. So is gonna be a little bit tough. But I think he has most of the chances is for Pete. After is coming, Guga, me, Yevgeny. But if he wants to win, nobody I think will complain.
