05/15/01 Tennis Master Series 2001 in Hamburg



M SAFIN/J Knippschild 6-2, 6-4

An Interview With: MARAT SAFIN

ATP: Questions, please.

Q. Well done. Do you feel you are starting to pick up a bit of form now?

MARAT SAFIN: Yeah, I am glad that I am backing in, trying to get my confidence back. I am feeling okay and I hope I will be perfect already this week, can make some results and I can play my best tennis as soon as possible. That is very important for me because I will need everything for French Open.

Q. Is that the best you have served since you were injured?

MARAT SAFIN: Yeah. I served much better. I feel okay and just I need to win some matches before the French Open.

Q. Before when you were serving not at full pace, was that still a physical problem or was it a mental?

MARAT SAFIN: No, more mental because I mean, I am okay actually. But sometimes muscles get tight because, I didn't work for actually for one month and a half, I couldn't do many exercises with my back because otherwise it gets worse and worse. So I tried to warm-up a little bit and then I start to serve full power and I think okay.

Q. Is the warming down also important for the back as well as the warming up?

MARAT SAFIN: Yeah, of course. If you don't take care of it -- it is a very special part of my body.

Q. I saw you flexing a little bit during the match. It was all right?

MARAT SAFIN: It is okay. I am happy. I am satisfied for the first round. Also the conditions are not so great. I mean, because it is cold, no sun, and courts are little bit soft so they bounce and baseline is a little bit, not very good because I think I am moving too much there and the courts are not -- not perfect.

Q. How much is Mats Wilander helping you and how much is he helping you mentally in particular?

MARAT SAFIN: I think it is very important to have the confidence that the coach give you confidence, enough confidence to make you play better and better even if you are playing not great tennis, he has to give you confidence because as soon as -- I mean, when start to give you confidence, nothing happens, okay, that is trying, not playing, okay, come on, you are playing okay, you start to realize actually you are not so bad. You get confidence much faster than when you are just too long down and just trying to win one match for few weeks. That is why I need Mats to give me confidence so can pass this period for one, two weeks maximum.

Q. Has he been with you at all between Key Biscayne and here because you have been injured?

MARAT SAFIN: I was with him one week practicing and now.

Q. Where was that?

MARAT SAFIN: In Monte Carlo.

Q. He is through with you now through the end of Wimbledon or what?

MARAT SAFIN: I don't know.

(Laughs) no, because I think he has some things to do and he is coming Thursday or Wednesday before French Open. So goes for two days and he is coming back.

Q. Have you felt you spent too long on the Tour maybe either on your own or with different people with you and no consistent companion?

MARAT SAFIN: Of course, every person is bringing something new and first of all, the person if you start to work with somebody you have to get to know him. You can't start working with a guy if you never talk to him and you don't know, he has to know how are you, who you are, actually, I mean, it takes a few weeks until you start to understand each other and start to working.

Q. What has been the lowest point for you in the last few weeks?

MARAT SAFIN: Monte Carlo. I mean, worse it cannot be. I think it was like no comments.

Q. In a way do you feel that you are actually starting your year again now?

MARAT SAFIN: It is a little bit too late, I think, to start the year, we are already in May and I will try to make -- difficult now to talk if I can finish No. 1, No. 2, Top-3 - very difficult because I mean, the guys like Andre, Kuerten, Ferrero is playing great tennis. It is very difficult to catch them. I hope that they have -- they can play normal tennis and wait for me a few weeks and after I can go with them, I don't know, forward. It is a little bit difficult. So I will try to -- I would like to make -- finish the year at least in the Masters, it would be great for me.

Q. Are you looking forward to the hard court season?

MARAT SAFIN: Yeah. I have to. The clay court is already finishing. It is like French Open, two weeks more. Then the grass coming so actually I have chance probably I will try to make some results there and then starting the USA again.

Q. You are breaking anymore rackets this year?

MARAT SAFIN: Yeah, but less.

Q. Much less?

MARAT SAFIN: Less. Less.

Q. Is that due in part to Mats Wilander?

MARAT SAFIN: Yeah, I get fined with him.

Q. How much?

MARAT SAFIN: $100.

Q. For each racket?

MARAT SAFIN: Yeah, it's enough, no?

Q. He wants you to break more?

MARAT SAFIN: I don't want to make him rich.

Q. When did you start your new contract with Dunlop?

MARAT SAFIN: Miami. They couldn't give me so much racket so I have to buy them.

Q. Any likelihood of you talking to Pickard during Wimbledon or not?

MARAT SAFIN: He is a good friend of mine, but I have a coach, I have to respect my coach. But I think it would be ---

Q. He knows Mats well.

MARAT SAFIN: Not a problem. But it's okay, I can -- now at least I have a coach, a stable coach and I am very glad and very happy so I'd like to spend all the time with him to learn. Also I have to make some results here so I have to listen to him.
