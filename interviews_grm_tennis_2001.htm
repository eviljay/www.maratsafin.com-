"Shouting, roar, curse - it belongs to me"


German Tennis magazine, April 2001
Interview with Marat Safin
translated by Julia


The Year 2000 was for Marat Safin the year of his discovery. The year 2001 is the year of demonstration of his worth. In tennis magazine the US Open champion speaks about his example Pete Sampras and his ideal trainer Mats Wilander.

tennis magazine: Mister Safin, Pete Sampras had described you as the future of tennis. How do you live with this compliment?
Marat Safin: Sounds good. A fat lot uf use that is.I have to see, that I can bring my results on the court.

tennis magazine: Do you have the feeling, that you are able to achieve the class of Sampras or Andre Agassi?
Marat Safin: I am pretty self-confident, but I do not think, that someone from our generation would achieve, what both of them had achieved. Pete and Andre are gods, against them we, the young players, did not have achieve anything.

tennis magazine: Why is it today harder, to stay for a longer time at the top of tennis?
Marat Safin: Agassi and Sampras are the last one, who dominated a whole era. Goodness, Sampras was for seven years the number one, this won't never again achieve anybody. Everything is so hurried. You can fall from the top and do not have to have even a crisis. These days the top one hundred player are able to beat each other at all time. There are no hierarchy anymore.

tennis magazine: But why Sampras and Agassi still win the big tournaments?
Marat Safin: They could acquire their ruthless and class at the big tournaments in an another time. Ten, actually five years ago there was a low in tennis, today vast numbers of top players, always ready to for a sensation. When you get through never farther than to the third, fourth or fifth round at the Garnd Slams, you are a good player, a very good player, but not a record man like Sampras.

tennis magazine: Does the insight weaken you, that tennis is so unpredictable and the challenge all the time get greater?
Marat Safin: I have the feeling, to learn the newest thing each day and each hour. What I can buy today on my laurels from the US Open winning?

tennis magazine: You have a better position at the sponsor poker. You just change your racket outfitter from Head to Dunlop. This you had gild for your own, certainly.
Marat Safin: Maybe. But sportily such a nice victory fade very fast. Particularly, because the nice colleagues look now exactly at that Safin. You were checked thoroughly. The surprise factor is gone.

tennis magazine: You knock down the pressure with sturdy racket smashes. You are the record holder in questions of racket consumption.
Marat Safin: I am not a type, who weave a game through like a paragon - quiet and soft. Shouting, roar and curse - it belongs to me. Today and in future. When I could not take out my fury and were not allowed to break a racket, I would get totally tense. And my nervousness higher.

tennis magazine: But at the beginning of 2000 when you almost wanted to stop with tennis, you broke the rackets by the dozen. At that time that had not got a therapeutic effect.
Marat Safin: No, for heaven's sake. I feel at that time like a boiler, which stood Constantly for an explosion. That was unhealthly. I mean only: No one can expect, that we drone our game like roboters. People regret, that the types in tennis die out. Than they have to accept oppositely, that people like me do something crazy.

tennis magazine: Do you see yourself more as a tennis pro or as an entertainer in a modern sport-show?
Marat Safin: The people like me, because I am not like many others. I leave my heart out on the court, my emotions. Did I do it for business reasons? No: I behave just like me. I do not have to feign. I bid a good show, but I am not a showman.

tennis magazine: After your US Open victory you exchanged your sportily escort, intensely. The last time actually an alliance with the former swedish number one, Mats Wilander, was under discussion.
Marat Safin: In general it is not so easy, to find good people, who can travel with you. Mats would be top-class partner, no doubt. But is he ready to work with me on a day-to-day-base? That we want to solve fast.

tennis magazine: How did you get in touch with him?
Marat Safin: That mediated a mutual friend. I was with him in the middle of december in St. Polten, he spoke to me over a glass of beer in a skibar: you, Mats is interested, to work with you. But I was at first reserved, I needed time to think over.

tennis magazine: What are there to think over?
Marat Safin: That had asked me my friend too. He meant: Such one like Mats you do not get every day. A little later I said to my myself: dammed, you are alone, you do not have at the time a real coach - you have to make use.

tennis magazine: What do you want, what do you have to improve with your trainer?
Marat Safin: Certainly my mental strength. 80% of the games were won in the head, athletic are all equal good, today. I think, you have to radiate a positive arrogance on the court, an attitude mediating class. Like Sampras. At Sampras you do not have the feeling, that he is deceptive condescendingly.

tennis magazine: Is it possible to learn these strengths?
Marat Safin: Until a certain grade: yes. A lot is a question of time. You learn at the rise with each match in the tennis circuit. Sometime you get a better understanding , what to do in a match.

tennis magazine: When Pete Sampras ward off a matchball with an ace, when top-players like Agassi conjures at the Bip Points his best shots, is this the result of this apprenticeship in the tennis life?
Marat Safin: Of course is this callous, which had Becker, which Sampras have, an innate quality, too. There are sensational players, who had their problems during tricky match situations: They do not get on the top. Nevertheless I say: You can learn to win.

tennis magazine: Now the clay-court tournaments in Europe are approaching . How do you see your chances?
Marat Safin: I like to play on clay-court, though it cost a lot of energy. But it is funny, to develop a strategy like a chess player. I wish long for a French Open triumph. It would be the greatest in this moment of my career.

tennis magazine: Is your love for clay-court linked with your tennis adoptive country Spain?
Marat Safin: Oh yes, I grew up their as a player. A nice, but a hard time. We played our hoarse, rummaged every day in sand, fighted like lions. That was a school, which formed myself. And the guys, with whom I was together, I still get along with them like with old class mates.

tennis magazine: But nevertheless you fond of your Russian home, of Moscow and their people, of Russia?
Marat Safin: I have Russian soul. I like to live in my country. As much as I want to win as a single player all Grand Slams, so much I wish to win one day the Davis Cup as Russian. Sooner or later we fetch these dammed bowl, Yevgeny Kafelnikov and me, for sure.
