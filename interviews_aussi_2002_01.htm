Australian Open - January 17, 2002 Melbourne, Australia
Marat Safin - Christophe Rochus 6-2, 7-6, 6-1.
An interview with: MARAT SAFIN


Q. Marat, last year you had the elbow, you had lots of problems; now there's all these guys this year are hurt. Are you healthy and do you think maybe it's fair you get some luck this time?

A. I think I'm the only one in the draw who is healthy, more or less, because as we can see everbody has a problem. So of course it is the beginning of the year and everybody was on vacations and I was working, so no vacations for me. I'm ready and I'm feeling well, healthy, and I want to do well here.

Q. Can you tell us about your work?

A. About my work? It is a secret. It is a special preparation. No, nothing special; just I had enough time between last year and this year just beginning, I had almost two months and I had enough time to go to vacations and enough time to get prepared for this year. I had almost four weeks of hard work and, as you can see - the girl who is behind you, she saw me - I was working hard and I'm doing well.

Q. When so many seeds go out and you are preparing for a match, it could be difficult, do you start thinking, "If I'm not spot on here I could be another one going out the door"?

A. You need to have this in your mind, so you have to play from the beginning until the end, until the last point, and that is what I did. And of course the second set wasn't the best set I ever played, but I was there and I saved four set points, and I played well, I think, great serve and volley, and I'm getting more confident, more and more. I'm ready to play and I'm ready to make good results here. I think I have a good chance.

Q. Just on that: given the number of seeds that go out, would you say this is your best opportunity of winning a Grand Slam in how long?

A. I don't know, because you still have other guys who are playing well, and the young guys that you didn't even heard about them but they are doing well and they are very dangerous, and everybody wants to do well here and everybody I think realised that all the big players are out. So also me, I want to do well here, it is a huge opportunity, but we have a very tough part of the draw: we have Roddick, we have Sampras, I have Escude and Youzhny, the biggest star in Russia, and it is difficult, you know. Everybody wants to win and everybody wants to do well.

Q. Marat, you mentioned that you have got to look out for the young guys: do you not consider yourself one of the young guys any more?

A. No, of course, I'm still young, I think - 21, in two weeks I'm going to be 22 - so I think I still can play tennis and I have very good motivation and I'm young and of course I want to win as many Grand Slams as I can. I have a good chance here, so why not here?

Q. Marat, is the fact that you were able to save those set points and get through a set like that a good sign that you are mentally strong and focused?

A. Yes, because you have to be careful if you lose one set here and you can be in big trouble, because it is five-set match and you have to fight; and Rochus, he is a tough guy, he can run forever and he has very good hands so he can drive you crazy on the court so you have to be careful against these kind of players.

Q. Would you say you are stronger in that department than you have been in the past?

A. Just - I'm trying to be focused, trying to stay - I have to be a tough guy on the court so everybody has to be scared of me, and everybody has to work a little bit harder than normally to beat me.

Q. Is Mats Wilander here?

A. No, he's not here.

Q. Do you still plan to work with him?

A. Yes, just he didn't come here, but I think we are still going to work, and I think he is coming to the States.

Q. Has your coaching situation sort of stabilised a bit over the last - - -

A. I have no problems. I have a big friend of mine, he is starting with me, and we are doing well I think. I'm playing good, we enjoy, you know, practising, we are doing well. Slowly we are getting closer and closer to the top 10, and then I think I have a chance to at least to finish the year No. 1, so we are doing everything right and I don't have any problems.

Q. Marat, obviously you have put last year behind you. After 2000, have you sort of found it a little bit difficult last year at times?

A. The problem is that the schedule was a little bit too tough for me, because we finished the Master's, like I said, in 2000 in the first week of December, so I need to have my vacations and I didn't have enough time to prepare myself for 2001. So of course I got injured straight away, and all the year was a disaster. But still I managed to win two tournaments, make quarter-finals in Wimbledon and semi-finals at US Open and I stayed in the top 20, I almost finished top 10 - I finished 11. So I think this year I will be much better.

Q. Marat, you and a lot of the other young guys have Sampras in your half of the draw; how much do you younger guys still respect his reputation and his ability?

A. I think everybody knows that Pete is the biggest ever. He won 13 Grand Slams and you have to respect his game. It is Pete Sampras and you have to play really well against him to beat him. You have to stay focused because you know that if you are not there on the court he will beat you very easy. He is very tough so we have to respect him.

Q. You said Youzhny is the biggest star in Russia: I thought the biggest star in Russia was Kournikova?

A. No, Youzhny is first.

Q. Why?

A. Well, just I know he is very good this guy. I practise with him and basically we grew up in the same club - Kournikova, Youzhny and me - so we have a very good club in Moscow and he is doing well. He is a hard worker and I think he has a great future because he is quite professional and he has a very good guy training with him and they are doing well and he will be really, really tough in the future. I think this year he will do well, he will be in the top 20.

Q. Who is the guy you are hitting with that you said? At the beginning you said you were practising with some guy?

A. That is another one.

Q. Who is the guy who is your coach or who is here with you?

A. My coach?

Q. The guy who is here with you?

A. Amit Naor, he was my manager before, he is, I don't know, coach, manager; everything together.

Q. With the top five seeds disappearing from this tournament, after being out on court a couple of times is there anything about the conditions, the court, anything at all that you can help explain this exodus of the top players?

A. I don't know. I can't explain. I think they didn't have enough time to rest or to prepare themselves, I think this may be the main reason, because everybody who played the Masters they are out, basically - Kuerten, Hewitt, Grosjean, Kafelnikov - everybody who were in the semi-finals in the Masters. Ferreira didn't even come here, he was injured already in Spain. So I think the reason is that they didn't have enough time to rest. Maybe that is the reason, I'm not sure.

Q. As someone who enjoys their vacations, would you like to have the tournament shifted back a bit? Would you like the tournament to be pushed back so that people could - - -?

A. No, no, I'm satisfied. I finished the year perfect. I finished the year at the beginning of November, I had enough time to rest, to go for vacations, to enjoy my life, and enough time to get ready for this year, to make a great preparation physically, mentally, and just enough time.

Q. And in between matches off the court is there anything that you are doing this year different to last year, anything at all? Are you spending your time just in the way of keeping focused on things?

A. I am not doing anything at all.

Q. Really?

A. Nothing. Zero.

Q. What do you like doing when you come here?

A. I don't know. I have too much energy. I don't know, too many things I have in my head, so maybe that's why I have never played well here because, you know, many things.

Q. No restaurants, no - - -?

A. No, of course I go to restaurants, but I take it very easy. I have my friends around and we stay at home, we watch tennis, we learn how to play tennis, just take it easy. I think that's the main thing. So the less things you have in your head, the better you play. You have to be just relaxed and enjoy your moment, enjoy your victories, and get prepared for the next match.

Q. Do you ever reflect on what happened two years ago here in the first round when you were fined?

A. Shit happens, you know. What can I say? It happens. I'm really sorry, and I think also they probably make a little bit big scandal out of this out, I think. Nobody were watching this match even, you know, and it was a bit depressing. But it was okay. I got fined, yes; I didn't play my best tennis, but it was two years ago, and I'm a different guy, a different player, and I'm ready to win this year, I think.
