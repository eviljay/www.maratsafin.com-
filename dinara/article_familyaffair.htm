FAMILY AFFAIR

Notes from the French Open 2000
Tuesday, 6 June 2000


For 14-year-old Dinara Safina, tennis is a family affair, Russian style.

Thirty-five years ago her mother, Rauza Islanova, was a junior semifinalist at the French Open.

On Wednesday, her brother, Marat Safin takes on Magnus Norman in the quarterfinals.

Safin predicts a bright future for his little sister, who reached the fourth round in junior play before falling Tuesday to Marie-Eve Pelletier.

"In two or three years, people won't be calling her Marat's sister, but they'll be calling me Dinara's brother," said 20-year-old Safin.

Islanova couldn't agree more. "Marat hadn't won anything by the age of 14," she told the French daily L'Equipe. "At 14, Dinara has won everything for her age."

Safina picked up her first racket at the age of 8 under the tutelage of her mother, a former junior champion who still remembers playing her semifinal match at Roland Garros on Court No. 5.

Safina practiced daily at Spartak, a small Moscow club with only one court.

The club is managed by her father, Michail, whose hoots and howls during sports events are legendary.

"I've the same hot temperament as my father," she said in halting English, adding that she feels more confident speaking Spanish.

No wonder. Now, the family spends half the year in Valencia, Spain, returning to Moscow in the summer to practice in cooler temperatures.
