18.05.2002
Tennis Masters Series Hamburg
Press conference with MARAT SAFIN

Safin versus Robredo 6:2, 4:2 (retired)

Question (Q): Were you aware that there was something wrong with him during the match?

A: I didn’t notice anything. He played okay in the first set. I thought he was moving well, but maybe he had some problems. For me it was a nice surprise that he retired.

Q: Can you sum up how this week was for you, particularly after your disappointing week in Rome?

A: I’m feeling very happy and satisfied. I’m going to be the second or first in the race. So I’m well prepared for Roland Garros. I’m moving well and serving well. I have to make sure I’ll be able to do the same again there than I did here.

Q: Would it be something special for you to be the number 1 in the race tomorrow?

A: It’s nice, it’s always nice. You always work hard to get to the first spot, especially being there at the beginning of the season. I made some good results so far, I was in the finals in Australia, then the quarter finals in Miami, quarter finals in Monte Carlo and now in the finals here. It’s good. I’m not defending anything until the end of the year. I would be satisfied to do the same for the rest of the season.

Q: You have been to the finals here before. Is there any difference between then and now?

A:No difference. I played really well two years ago, I was moving very well and I was full of confidence. Now I’m just climbing up and I try to get to the point where I was in 2000. I’m nearly there. I hope I can play much better that I did two years ago. I’m looking forward to the finals and I hope I’ll be fast and calm and make the match. That would be very nice.

Q: What were the biggest problems for you when you played Roger the last two matches on clay?

A: The first set was very tough, it was 7:6 in the third and I was coming up after an injury, it was in Rome last year and it was my second match on clay. Then I played him again this year and I didn’t play very well, and he beat me quite easily. But it’s a different story tomorrow, it’s five sets and I have a lot of time, I can spend as many hours as I need to. I’m enjoying it to stay here and I don’t mind to stay there for five hours if I need that to beat him. I’ll be there and I will try to play my best tennis.

Q: You were in the finals here two years ago. Are you a better player now than you were then?

A: Of course. You learn more things with time, you improve your game. I serve better now, I think this week I served really well. There is definitely more experience in my back.

A: Marat, how did you come to team up with Amit Naoa, was it something you planned for a long time?

A: Yes, for a long time. Some people think I just met him when I started to play well, but that is not true. I know this guy since I started playing at a professional level. He has been my friend for a long time, five or six years. He helped me a lot when I was down and didn’t make any result, he was with me then. At the end of last season we decided to work together. You know, he was my manager before, and he knows me quite well, he knows what I need and he’s making my life easier. I really appreciate that. He’s a really good friend and I’m happy to have him next to me.

Q: Do you expect Marc to tell you a few secrets about Roger?

A: I’ve also spent a few years on the tour and I know how to play against Roger, he also knows how to play against me. So there are not many secrets Marc can tell me. I think I know quite well what I have to do on the court (laughs).

Q: What can you say about Roger’s weaknesses?

A: I mean, if I would tell you we would be still at it tonight (laughs). There are some points, because everybody has some weaknesses and you have to push them. You have to stay with him, even if it takes five hours. You have to be strong mentally, then you definitely have your chances. If you are not able to take them, then you have a problem. But I think I’m playing good enough to beat him.
