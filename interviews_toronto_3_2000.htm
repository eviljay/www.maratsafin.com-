After the match in the final (M. Safin - H.Levy)
at Tennis Masters Series in Toronto Sunday, 6th of August 2000

After capturing the title

TOUR OFFICIAL: Okay. Marat will move from 8th to 4th on the ATP Championships Race when they are released tomorrow. This is his first Tennis Masters Series title and he is the first Russian to win here since Andrei Chesnokov, in 1991. First question for the champion?

REPORTER: Marat, I think you impressed Harel with your power and the speed of your serves. And I think you impressed a lot of people watching you in the crowd. I think Pete Sampras alluded to the fact earlier this week that you are potentially a number one player in the world some day. Are you feeling that you're getting there?

M. SAFIN: First of all, I think the serve is very important. So if sometimes you try to serve as fast as you can to, you know, to win the point very fast. Of course, it's very risky, because normally the serve is -- when you go serving hundred per cent, full power, normally, it will go far or to the net, because there's less control. But sometimes, when you're confident, so sometimes it works.Also, I mean, what you said about Sampras?


REPORTER: In an interview with Pete, Pete said that you would some day be number one or had the potential to be number one. I wonder if you today feel that is a realistic chance maybe this year?

M. SAFIN: No, actually this year I think it's impossible. Because I mean the guys like Norman, like Kuerten, they are too good. One guy won Roland Garros, and the other guy made the semifinal of Australia, final of Roland Garros. It is a big difference in the points. I mean they will not drop very easily in other tournaments. They're playing better and better each time. Just, I hope and I will be really happy to be in the top 5, it will be the best. I don't want expect for too much. Because at the beginning of the year, I didn't win one match. For me to win my match was something incredible. But now just -- at that time, like seven months ago, I set out to become top 30, top 20 player. Now I'm thinking about top 5. But number one I think I have a little bit of time, I need one or two years, you know, to be like -- I think for next year I can fight for it. When Pete and Andre will not play any more, maybe I have a chance. But we have also other guys like Norman, like Kuerten so you have start to play tennis since the first week of the year to play at a great level. Then you can talk about number one in the world. Not when you drop six months doing nothing to become number one in the world. It doesn't work like this.

REPORTER: Marat, aside from the match where there was a retirement, this is the first one you've played this week that didn't go three sets. But would you say it was the first match this week?

M. SAFIN: My best match, I think was against Pete. I was completely in the match. I was from the first point until the end I tried to return. I was pretty confident of what I was doing. I was doing, I tried to return everything, I tried to run, to get all the balls so he knows that I'm going to fight until the end. So I put him -- it's more pressure, because he has to put the volley perfectly, otherwise I will get them. So I think also I returned very well and I serve, so I didn't give him a lot of chances to break me. I mean, other matches it was different, because you know, I played not my best tennis mentally, of the confidence because with Ferreira I couldn't put one volley in the third set. I didn't know what to do with the balls. I didn't -- I had eyes like this big and I was shocked. So it was just a question of be patient. First two matches was more or less the same, I didn't play well, but I waited until the other guys, they missed. More or less it was I think against Pete it was a great match. Today, I have more experience than he has, he gets a little bit nervous, sometimes he doesn't know where to go. So it was the question of experience, nothing else, because he is playing unbelievable tennis. His forehand, backhand, volley, serve, everything. But in the final, it's normal, you know, that you go to a final, you pass the quali, you play chances before, to become everybody's talking about you, in the centre of attention it's a little tough.

REPORTER: Marat, apart from your great experience, would you say the dominating factor of the match today was your serve?

M. SAFIN: Of course the serve it gives you confidence, yes. But when he broke me in the first game, I was a little bit, "Ooh-la-la, today I'm going to fight a lot. I'm going to run." But after I break him, it was really, I knew how I had to play. And it wasn't a question of serve. I think I could play without so big serve, like over 200. But sometimes you need it, you know, to keep him under pressure all the time. But, I mean, good serve gives you confidence. It makes your game much easier. Because he knows always that if he doesn't return now, the next time will be another serve of 200 kilometres per hour. So you try to, under pressure, he knows all the time he has to be on the return, thinking about his. It is always, I need to return, I need to return. It is a little bit like this.

REPORTER: Marat, now that you've won your first Masters Series title and gotten to the top 5, how satisfied are you with the progress of your game this far?

M. SAFIN: I mean, I came here, I couldn't play tennis. You can ask Marcelo. I came here, the first day I came I practiced against him. He won. The score was 6-1, 6-2. He beat me in the practice. I didn't know where the ball was going, I didn't know how to run, I didn't know how to serve, I couldn't do anything. The problem is, not the problem -- the solution is when you're playing very bad, to become the Masters Series finalist is a small turn, it's nothing else. You don't have to work months. It's a question of one or two days, you become -- you look with other eyes at your mistakes. Because normally, when people play bad they say, I'm playing bad, I cannot play any more. Because it's a question of just two days. Just be concentrated not for hours, you can play the final for one. I didn't do anything special, I didn't eat vitamins in my life. I just, I came here, played horrible, I start to practice, I had to focus a little bit and, you know, I'm in the final. It's quite simple, a question of work.

REPORTER: Marat, you seem to have much more control in your temperament than you did a year or two ago. Can you explain, please, how you have done that and what do you need to do more to get control of your game?

M. SAFIN: Control of the head or of the game?

REPORTER: Head you already know, game for the future.

M. SAFIN: Definitely I have become a little bit relaxed on the court. I can control my situation. I can analyse the situation when -- if I'm losing, you know, because, of course it's very important to be relaxed on the court, be confident. Otherwise, if you get very nervous, you cannot control, you cannot hold the situation on the court when you're losing. So it's, of course, you start to miss much more. So you have to try from the beginning to be relaxed in the court. Of course, you can smash the racquet, but it's not a question. You still have to fight and try to find your game and, you know, be a little bit under control. Your head must be -- must control your body. Don't let your body do one thing and your head another thing. Otherwise there's no chance to win one match. Just have to be a little bit concentrated and try to listen, you know, what is, your mind, I think.

REPORTER: How do you do that? Do you prepare?

M. SAFIN: No, I don't prepare, I just practice for five hours. Finally, you can start to listen your mind. Otherwise, it is a question of hours, of experience, of long matches, you become much more clever and you start to realize a situation that you have to be relaxed, you have to listen to your head. What is happening, you try to calm down on the court. You try to do everything is like fighting, because otherwise everything will let calm. It's a very question of what is happening in your mind, you calm down, you fight, and everything will calm.

REPORTER: Is that the most important thing for the future, for controlling the strokes as well?

M. SAFIN: Of course. If you control -- if you control -- mind can control the body, everything going to work. Even if you play with the left hand, the question is you have to know what you're doing, just have to be confident there. So you're confident with the strokes, with the hands, with the legs. It's much more easier, tennis.

REPORTER: Marat, I just want to talk a little bit about your personality, the two sides. Sometimes you're temperamental, sometimes you're funny. And I think the crowd, I think, enjoys a show and sometimes you give them a show. And I wonder if you feel there's a little bit of the showman in you and whether the fans will see more of that in the future and whether you feel it's important to put on a show when you're out there?

M. SAFIN: What is important how to be on the court or not? I mean, everybody on tour, you have different personalities. I mean, Hewitt, Kuerten, Norman, Sampras, Agassi, me, everybody is different. Nobody is the same. So you never have to don't -- you never have to try. At least what I do, I don't want to be, you know, to try to do something unbelievable, to show the people in a different way. I'm like this and I'm talking here like I am I am in life, like I am in normal life, because I mean if I'm working. If you feel stupid, if you want to make people think that you are clever, you are a smart guy, you know. I mean you have to behave like you are in life the easiest way. The people they are, I think the people they understand this. I hope so.

REPORTER: My name is Maja, I have a question. You played in Slovenia two years ago. Do you have nice memories, do you remember?

M. SAFIN: Where?

REPORTER: In Slovenia, two years ago.

M. SAFIN: Yeah, a lot of points I get there. It was good.

REPORTER: So nice memories?

M. SAFIN: Yeah, because before there, it was two years ago I played the Challenger. So I get a lot of points there, I made the semifinal. So I was very close to being top 100. There it was one of my best tournaments and my best results.

REPORTER: And I read this morning that you are a big soccer fan. Did you watch European Cup, in June?

M. SAFIN: European Cup? Euro 2000, I didn't watch, because Russia, unfortunately, they're too good in football, that's why they didn't play in the final draw.

REPORTER: And I was wondering, do you like parties, and are you going to throw one this evening because you won today? Are you going to have a nice big party today?

M. SAFIN: Party? Unfortunately I have to go to Cincinnati, otherwise, I would invite you. But I have to leave to Cincinnati. After New York, I can talk about party. But now I have to be concentrating on my business.

REPORTER: How do you spend your free time?

M. SAFIN: How do I spend? I spend it at the tennis club, I spend it in the hotel, in the car and sometimes go to the cinema, but, I mean, life, this is your life. I mean, there's nothing special when I'm not doing -- many things, we cannot do it because we play a match, you have to come here, have you to eat, you stay most of the time at the club, at the site. After, if you have time, or you -- normally, you are dead after the match, so you have to be relaxed, you come to hotel, you go to eat for dinner. Then you can go to the cinema, can you have a cup of whatever you want, you can go to the bar. If you can, you can go to disco, but it's very difficult, because you get tired. If you do you spend too much time you get tired of the situation, of all this atmosphere. It's like all jobs, you get tired of everything quick. You get tired physically and mentally. So you need to relax. Sometimes you don't want to go anywhere, you just want to be in a room because this is your second house.

REPORTER: Marat, some people think you didn't do so well at the French Open. You were playing very well before, you played every week, every week, every week. For the U.S. Open, are you do anything differently so you maybe won't be quite as tired when the U.S. Open starts?

M. SAFIN: Right question. I think I made a big mistake before Roland Garros. I played too many tournaments. But why I played too many tournaments? Because I signed before when I was 14th in the world and I had to play as many matches as I can to get my confidence back. So I cannot say when I won Barcelona, "No, thank you guys, I don't play any more here, I play the tournament, I am the best." Because if you promise, you have to play the tournament. Of course, when you won the tournament, you win something more. You win another tournament, you win another tournament, it's not possible. So what happen, at the final in Hamburg, I played Duseldorff, I was completely dead. I played four matches and I came to Roland Garros, okay. I played four sets, five sets, another five sets. I had to play Magnus Norman, I couldn't play, my legs were exhaust. I hope I will not do it this year, I have to be a little bit clever. But it's going in the same situation, I know, it's a big same mistake. Same situation, but I will try to have less people around me it's better. You don't have to make a lot of people, you don't have to give attention to everybody. I will try to be a smaller family and be only with them. I know that some people, they don't understand this because they want to talk to you, they want to be friends with you. But for our job, it's a little bit difficult because you get tight in the head, you can't play. So it's small family, I go U.S. Open. And after we'll see.

REPORTER: I have a question regarding your coaching situation. Why did you split with Chesnokov and will Volkov become your permanent coach for the future?

M. SAFIN: With Andrei Chesnokov, what happened was one small misunderstanding. We're still friends and I don't want to lose him like a friend. For me it's more important. With Alexander, I don't want to lose him as a friend. So that's why we're not working together. Also he has a business in Russia. With Cherkasov, also I don't want to lose him as a friend. So it's a little bit difficult situation. But, I mean, it's more important that you have friends. So I have to look for a coach for a serious guy -- I don't know, normal coach, professional coach.

REPORTER: Do you have someone in mind?

M. SAFIN: No, I don't want to think about this, because when you start think, you go crazy. I have to play. I can play alone, of course with the help of Alexander. But, I mean, I will think about this after the U.S. Open. For me it's more than important these tournaments and conclude the U.S. Open. So after then I can think and choose.

REPORTER: During the match I noticed that you were talking a lot to yourself. What do you tell yourself in order to get your focus back?

You try to explain to yourself that you are stupid, that you don't have to do like this next time. But I think it's not normally, the concentration you get from other point. But normally, when you talk to yourself, you say, "I love you, you're a good guy, but don't miss next time, okay." More or less like this. In other words.
