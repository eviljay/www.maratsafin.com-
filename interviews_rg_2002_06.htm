Friday, June 7, 2002, Roland Garros
Marat Safin - J.C. Ferrero 3-6, 2-6, 4-6

Q. If on a scale of 1 to 100 you could rank your mental strength in this tournament, it was probably somewhere in the 90s for the first five matches. Where would you say it was for this match?

MARAT SAFIN: No, it's just I think the guy was much better than me. I try to play, but I didn't -- I couldn't find my game. He was just too good. I think he was playing with a lot of confidence and he was serving well. He moved me around the court. I couldn't do actually much. That's why the score was 6-2, 6-3, 6-4. I tried, but I couldn't.

Q. So what happened in the past times when you beat Ferrero? What could you do then that you couldn't do today?

MARAT SAFIN: No, I was -- I don't know. But today just I couldn't find my game. And when I had a break in the first set, but the guy, he played much better than before. And he has more experience now. He won two Super 9's -- two Masters Series events. He made already last year semifinals here. The guy, you know, he knows how to play. And he just -- he learned a lot. Now he's very dangerous. And he was just much better than me. I couldn't say that actually I played so bad. But just I couldn't find the solution to beat him.

Q. So do you think he will be the clear favorite for the final?

MARAT SAFIN: Who?

Q. Juan Carlos.

MARAT SAFIN: I don't know, because Costa is playing well, also. He made results in the clay court season. He made quarterfinals of Monte-Carlo. He made finals in Barcelona. Then he made in Rome I think quarterfinals. He beat Ferrero in Hamburg. He's a dangerous player. But I think is going to be tough finals. And, I don't know, it's finals, so you never know who going to be the winner.

Q. What do you recall of your relationship with Juan Carlos when you were practicing together near Valencia?

MARAT SAFIN: No, just I think all you can say. I don't know, he started a little bit later than me, you know, like one year later. But the guy, you know, he can play -- but he played completely different when he was a kid. I mean, he's hitting the ball - before he couldn't hit the ball, he was just making lobs all the time, he never missed the ball. But now the guy is attacking, he's serving well. He changed a lot since that I saw him first time when I was 14. So it's just pretty impressive how you can change your game.

Q. Except Ferrero's game, do you know any other reason why you couldn't find your game?

MARAT SAFIN: Just, I don't know, I wasn't good enough today as I was. That's it. It's another day. You have to accept these things. You cannot play all the time your best tennis. Sometimes you're playing not great, you try, but you cannot find it. Of course, it's tough. But this life, it's how it is, that's sport. You have to, you know, be tough and just move over.

Q. What was the most missing in your game today?

MARAT SAFIN: I think the serve. I didn't serve really well. And just I was in the middle of the match, I was a little bit lost. You know, I didn't know what basically to do. And just I didn't feel really comfortable on the court, and I couldn't find the solution. That's why it was -- that's why the score 6-2, 6-3, 6-4, it's quite easy. I couldn't find the solution, I couldn't find the game. It was very difficult for me. I tried just to stay closer to the baseline and just move him around. But the guy, he was everywhere, and he was playing with a lot of confidence. That's tough to beat the player which is playing really well - especially Ferrero, he's moving fast and has great shots.

Q. What happened in the last game?

MARAT SAFIN: I thought it was at 30-All (smiling). I was gone. I was too concentrated in the match (laughter).

Q. On a similar note, you have missed a number of his service. How difficult was it to return and try to have the return ace on his serve?

MARAT SAFIN: Just one more time, a little bit louder.

Q. How difficult Ferrero's serve?

MARAT SAFIN: How difficult was Ferrero's serve?

Q. Yes. How difficult was it for you to hit it back in order to get a return ace?

MARAT SAFIN: The problem is not I think actually the serve, because you cannot just return the ball. You have to put it somewhere so he cannot attack. So basically you have to start the point without any, you know -- without giving advantage to Ferrero. Because if you serve, you have already advantage. But also if you just return the serve, and he's already with a forehand, he's just moving you around. So especially it's not a good solution. So you have to return that way. The guy, he has to start the point again. So basically you're in the same position. And then it starts the rally. It's a special tennis example. It's difficult to return when the guy has huge forehand and you know he will push you and will move you around the court. So then you are under pressure.

Q. In the second set, you were serving at 2-4, breakpoint against you. You hit a great serve into the corner of the ad side. He tossed up a very high lob. You went almost into the doubles alley. What happened there?

MARAT SAFIN: What happened there? I think what happened, I don't think the ball was going out actually because he was going like this way. But you have to take a risk, you taking the ball or you just let it bounce. So I decided take a risk and just hit a smash. And that was a bad solution. But, I mean, at that moment I thought it was the right one.

Q. Was it a good risk on breakpoint, on a ball that high?

MARAT SAFIN: I think everybody has own opinions about that. I think -- because otherwise with what you start? You restart the point, right? I don't want this, because I was already having advantage. I had a smash. Okay, I miss it. But if I don't miss it, then you will not ask me this question, right? Why you miss it? You have to ask the question, "Why you miss this point?" I will tell you. It's because the ball was too high and then it was too far away from the net and then is breakpoint. So you already have all these things on your mind. So that's why I miss.

Q. Some people say he has two forehands.

MARAT SAFIN: Huh?

Q. You see what I mean? He has two powerful sides, no difference between the backhand and the forehand. He's able to hit both. What do you think about that?

MARAT SAFIN: Everybody has a weak points. Is almost impossible to have two the same shots, if any forehand and backhand. It doesn't exist in tennis, right? It never exist. It's just rules, it's written in the book. It's from the beginners. Okay, one day you can play better forehand, another day you can play a better backhand. He has a very good backhand, but he doesn't make so much that -- but he does a lot with the forehand. He opens the court very good with the forehand. But with the backhand, he's playing more straight. I think his forehand much better. But his backhand is solid. That's the problem. Is solid. Just doesn't make any mistakes. Doesn't go for too much. And that's why it looks so good.

Q. At one point near the end of the match, you had a pretty long conversation with your racquet. I know normally you're not so friendly to your racquets. I was wondering if it did any good?

MARAT SAFIN: What answer are you looking for (smiling)? What answer you are looking for asking me this question?

Q. Are you usually more abusive to your racquet. This time you decided to be more gentle?

MARAT SAFIN: When you are losing, two sets to love down, and you trying just to put a return or just put it back, the ball, deep, and you cannot make it, what a conversation you can have with the racquet or with yourself? What conversation you can have (laughter)?

Q. That's what I want to ask you.

MARAT SAFIN: I give you the answer, but I don't think is the right one. But still...I was pissed. As you can see, I think so. I was very disappointed, so I need to do something because it was going for too much already. I could explode. But then it's bad for the public.

Q. You have opportunities to get to the finals of Grand Slams at this point in your career, but the older you get, obviously the less they come. Are you aware that they are very rare?

MARAT SAFIN: You have to look from other side also. You know, okay, like I'm 22, I made finals in Australia, I won the US Open two years ago, a long time ago. And I don't know if it will come. Yes, I was very close to be in the finals. Yes, I was close to win the tournament. But it's, you know, like from this point to be a winner in a Grand Slam, it's like from here to the moon. You know, you have to be tough, you have to play great, you have to be confident on the court. I didn't have all this points to be able to become a winner. And then, I don't know, I'm not sure that I'm going to be a winner one more time. Maybe yes, then it's great. If not, it's not a big deal. But just a sport which I am trying to enjoy myself every time I go to the court. Is difficult to do sometimes, like today. But, you know, I just want to play tennis. I want to enjoy it. If it will come, if I will become a Grand Slam winner again, I don't know, it would be a great moment in my life. But it's really, really difficult - as you can see.

Q. Do you have a specific goal for this season?

MARAT SAFIN: Yeah. I have two Grand Slams left, so I will try to, I don't know, be solid. Maybe I have a chance this year. Of course, I think -- I'm already Grand Slam champion two years ago, finals I have, so I think I'm missing No. 1. If I will be able to stay solid this year, make my results, slowly without rushing anything, then maybe I will have this opportunity. It will be great. I think my goal is to become No. 1.

Q. Do you foresee any decisions concerning coaches, or is it still a temporary situation?

MARAT SAFIN: I'll be honest. It's difficult to find the coach. I tell you what's reasons. First reason is that, you know, big players, big guys, they've been in Top 10, you know, they have families, the guys like over 30, right? So they have families. So it's difficult to have this guy for traveling for 42 weeks a year with you. It's very difficult. And on other hand is also that is difficult to find the coach who will understand your game, who will understand who you are, who you want to be, and what -- which way you want to play.

Q. You have to be Russian to understand your mentality?

MARAT SAFIN: Yes. We've been watching different cartoons. You think this way. We've been watching different cartoons. We grew up in a different system than Europeans. So it's very difficult to the European people, to understand the Russian one. Nobody can actually understand Russians. It's difficult. You can, but it's difficult, right? I think it's easier to work with a Russian guy. He more or less understands what you need, he understands how you think, what is your game, and what you want to be, what you need for your game. Because like it's a different school. French school is one school, Spanish school is a different one. So it's not so easy to find the right guy who will be able to travel with you, you know, also to bring the results. Of course, I mean, for the moment I am doing well with Amit Naor. He's helping me a lot. For the moment I don't want to rush. It's slowly. It will come. I hope so.
