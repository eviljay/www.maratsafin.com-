Wednesday, May 29, 2002, Roland Garros
Marat Safin - M. Llodra, 6-4, 2-6, 7-6, 6-4


Q. It looked like a tough game. What was happening on court?

MARAT SAFIN: Yeah, he played I think well. He's a tough player with these conditions - small court, crowd, a little bit of wind. His game is perfect to play with such conditions. He served quite well, very good volleys. But at the end I think the key was in the third set, 7-6, I won the tiebreak. From there, you know, I felt a little bit more of confidence, that I could return the serve and I could do a little bit more on his serve. It's very difficult, very difficult to play against these kind of guys.

Q. It looked like at certain points you were getting emotional, a little angry. In the third set, it looked like you were channeling your anger, really put it to use. Is that what you do?

MARAT SAFIN: I was a little bit frustrated because I had my chances, I couldn't just realize them. I was quite disappointed. But then I calmed down and I just, you know, start to play better, I start to have some more confidence. That helped me.

Q. Are you satisfied with your game on the clay? You're one of the players that doesn't matter of the surface, no?

MARAT SAFIN: Especially now is difficult to play on clay and win easy matches because everybody's playing quite well. They have very good game to play on any surface. Every match you win, you have to be happy. You have to say thanks that you passed the round because everybody can give you trouble.

Q. You're coming back from a back injury, a pinched nerve. At times it looked like you were rubbing. Is that giving you problems?

MARAT SAFIN: I couldn't really serve for two sets, I think. I didn't really serve hard. I played with a second serve. But then I decided because I have to push a little bit more with the serve. But I was a little bit scared. That's why I just didn't really serve well for the first two sets, and then I start to serve well and my game came up.

Q. Do you think it's going to be a problem in upcoming matches?

MARAT SAFIN: I hope not. I have to check my back after each match and see how it's going. Just I hope it will be okay in a few days.

Q. Is it continued good your relation with Marc Rosset?

MARAT SAFIN: Yeah. I think before to start to work with somebody, you have to know each other much better. So he needs time, I need time to get to know him better so we can do something big.

Q. You're No. 1 in the Champions Race now. You look at that? Does it matter the ranking so much for you?

MARAT SAFIN: No, I don't really. You have to look at the ranking the end of the year. During the year it's a lot of changes. Every time somebody is doing something big, the ranking is changing very fast. I think you have to check your game first and be careful with injuries, you know, just try to be focused, try to be stable for all the year and be consistent. Then the ranking is going to be okay because the ranking is coming with your game.

Q. You look like you've improved in this consistency. Final in Melbourne, final in Masters Series.

MARAT SAFIN: I didn't lose one first round this year, so it's quite good. I'm doing well. I'm trying to be focused, trying to be consistent. That's main thing. I think in the long run, it's going to help me.

Q. How much is important Davis Cup for you? Yevgeny say it the most important thing for him this year. Maybe if he wins the Davis Cup, he would like to retire. Is important for you? Are you going to help him?

MARAT SAFIN: I am far from retiring. But it's very important for us, yes, yes, it is, because we never won. We've been twice in the finals, '95 and '96. It would be great if we can win this year. It's quite difficult because still we are favorites against Argentina, but also the finals, it's tough. If France win, we're playing here in Roland Garros. I don't think we have a lot of chances. But if we beat Argentina, we play against the States, then I think we are favorites because we will play at home with our crowd and probably we're going to win.

Q. In France Wednesday is a day off in school. There were a lot of kids today.

MARAT SAFIN: I could notice it.

Q. Kind of noisy audience. What do you think?

MARAT SAFIN: Kids are kids. You cannot blame them. They're coming, they're having fun, you know, just shouting on the crowd. Is also good. Is good entertainment for them because they're future of your country, so they have to do something. They're playing tennis, they like football. They do different things, they have to be busy. I think if the people are coming, they're enjoying to watching tennis, I think it's very good. They're coming, a lot of them. It's good entertainment for them. I'm happy.

Q. You have to play Olivier Rochus in the second round. Do you know him? What do you know about his game?

MARAT SAFIN: I know him quite well. He's one year younger than me. I know him since I was 14. Very good player. He beat me once two years ago. Actually, it was last year in the beginning of the year. It was three sets in the Hopman Cup on the hard courts. He's a small guy, but he's very talented, very good hands, very fast, very good anticipation. He's dangerous on clay. He's always dangerous.

Q. What do you have to take care of?

MARAT SAFIN: Just play your game. I think my game is good enough to beat him. It's going to be difficult, but still I have to manage to find my game - you know, play confident, try to build the points, serve, everything.

Q. A question related to the crowd. When you're down, you hear the crowd chanting your opponent's name, you're playing against a French guy, how distracting is that?

MARAT SAFIN: It's the same thing happens in Russia. They supporting me in Russia. It's normal they support the French player. It would be strange if they support me. I think it's logical and it's okay. I think I can survive. It's just a game. You get pissed, you know, with your game, with all the things around you. But I think it's okay. Just a game anyway. You don't have to make such a big deal out of it.
