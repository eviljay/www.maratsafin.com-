November 18, 2000
M. SAFIN - Juan Carlos Ferrero 6-2,6-2
An interview with: MARAT SAFIN

Marat Safin eliminated Juan Carlos Ferrero in straight sets to advance to the final in Paris.


MODERATOR: Questions, please.

Q. Your tennis was very good. I'm just wondering about your mathematics now. We're trying to work out whether this performance here means that you are at the head of the entry system rankings now. Do you know that?

MARAT SAFIN: You mean if I'm No. 1 in the world?

Q. Not just the race, but the entry system.

MARAT SAFIN: Yes, because as everybody knows, the entry system there is a -- the points that are going with the -- Pete, he won the tournament in Hannover. So Monday of this week is -- the ATP, these guys, they gonna take all the 600 points. So mathematically, I'm gonna be No. 1. But first of all, it's nice. I'm satisfied that a lot of people helped me to be there at the top, but also it will be unbelievable if I'm gonna finish the year. But everybody knows that we're always fighting for -- everybody's playing great tennis here today. Magnus, I think he had some opportunities, but I'll be there. And now I would like to finish the year No. 1.

Q. Given the way that you started the year, could you ever have envisioned being in this position at this time of this year?

MARAT SAFIN: Definitely I would not -- I didn't think about this, because I start the year, it was horrible. I didn't think that I'm gonna even be in the Top 20. Now I'm fighting for No. 1. I'm playing great tennis with a lot of confidence, and it's great. I'm satisfied that finally I just -- I came back for the better ranking, to be there and fight during the whole summer, playing great tennis and fight for No. 1.

Q. This means that in the beginning of next year, whatever you do, you will remain No. 1. For the first three months, you have nothing to lose.

MARAT SAFIN: I'll be glad. I would like. But I know that it's tough. It's tough, because you have to play every week. But you know each week that everybody wants to be there on the first draw: Guga, Magnus, Pete, other guys, Yevgeny, they gonna fight for it. You have to, you know, to be better than them.

Q. First three months, you don't need to.

MARAT SAFIN: Yes, I do. Yes, I need. Guga's defending, also, I think, in Australia. So from the beginning of the year, they gonna fight to be there on the top. Who's gonna be stronger? I want to be there.

Q. You were hitting the ball really strongly today. Is that the best you've played since the US Open final?

MARAT SAFIN: Probably, yes. But also, like I said before, it's, you know, Juan Carlos, he didn't play his best tennis. He was too tired. I saw it, I mean the first point. He didn't arrive good to the ball. He made many mistakes. The concentration wasn't the same. It's difficult to make a good tennis. So I just had to make my game. And knowing this, that he's not playing his best tennis, it gives you more confidence. You play more relaxed because you know that each moment that you gonna break him and that you are better than him because you have more, you know -- physically, you are better this day, today.

Q. It seemed that you seemed to have perfected the Pete Sampras slam dunk today.

MARAT SAFIN: Yes, I learn from him. From so many times on the TV.

Q. You played much better than at the beginning of the indoor season. What adjustment did you make in your game during all those weeks you played since Vienna?

MARAT SAFIN: I think the confidence is the question. It's when you have a confidence, you can play great tennis. When you don't have confidence, you are losing, you know, first -- one week you're losing the first round, second week, you are already afraid, you know, of playing. Sometimes you think twice when you hit the ball. You think, "Ohhh, it's gonna be in or it's gonna be out?" And you don't know. You lose again, and you're losing confidence. Because to lose the confidence is very easy. To get the confidence back, it's very tough. So what happen? Olympic Games I lost to Santoro. Next week I lost in Vienna to Escude. Already, confidence -- is no confidence at all. You have to start from the zero, playing points by points, and try to find your confidence. And finally in St. Petersburg I found it in the final. Even losing, it was a tough match. I won it. And here I'm playing great tennis, I think. And I hope I'm gonna play the same way tomorrow in the final.

Q. Tomorrow you could actually become the only player to win two Masters Series this year. Does it mean anything to you?

MARAT SAFIN: Of course. It's nice. Also, it gives me -- it will give me points to go little bit forward from Guga, from Pete, and it's gonna be one more tournament for my tennis career.

Q. Talking about Ferrero, does he have the "Marat Safin complex" now?

MARAT SAFIN: I don't think so. Just all the time we're playing against each other, I'm just more confidence, playing better. That's it. Today he was tired. I don't think we'll have to count this match, because it wasn't equal. Because he was too tired. That's it.

Q. All the players who became No. 1 said in the past that it was more difficult to stay up there than to reach that goal. What would you be afraid of more about yourself being at the top: To lose consistency, to lose concentration, to have no more desire to fight or to work, whatever?

MARAT SAFIN: I think it's already big mistake to think -- to be afraid. Be afraid of what? Because if you gonna go down in the ranking, that's life. Everybody wants to be there on the first draw. I think you have to still playing and still count the point, you know, not to defend, "Oh, next week I have to defend the final of Rome," or, "I have to defend the final of Australia," or the winner of Scottsdale. That's not the point. You're No. 1. You have to count all the points. Each time, every tournament you have to give all your best; and if you are lucky, if you are, you know, in good shape, you gonna be there. But also, everybody have to be there. Nothing gonna happen if you go No. 3, because always you can come back. You don't have to be afraid to be No. 1.

Q. I shouldn't have used the word "afraid," but what would worry you more? Would you have to fight against yourself in order to stay up there?

MARAT SAFIN: Another thing, you don't have to fight against yourself. You gonna say it's enough to fight against Kuerten, against Philippoussis. You have to enjoy the life. No. 1 in the world, maybe it's the last time in your life until your tennis career. You have to enjoy it. If it's one week, that's great. You have to play there, you have to play each tournament thinking that, "I want to be No. 1 for a long time." Not for one week and you go. But also, I mean, one week at No. 1, it's great.

Q. You talked about Philippoussis. What's your thought on his game and on tomorrow's final?

MARAT SAFIN: His -- I mean Mark is in a good shape. He's playing very good because he passed through some tough matches against Dominik Hrbaty. It's a difficult match. If you lose, you lose. If you win this match 7-6 in the third, it's already you gonna be in the Finals -- semifinals for sure. I mean, in tennis, it's like this. You win one match, one tough match playing badly, and after you're playing better and better with a lot of confidence. Because these kind of matches give you confidence. So he's playing now unbelievable tennis. It's great. It's perfect. Serve, volley, baseline is great. But tomorrow is final, so you never know what is gonna happen there. I'm going to fight. I'm gonna build my game since the first point.And if I will have any chances to beat him, I will try to, you know, to push him and put him under pressure. It's gonna be the key of the match: To push him since the beginning of the match. And let's see what's gonna happen with Mark.

Q. Before this year, you had only one title in Boston. Now you have a nice collection. Do you think that you play the Finals now differently than you used to play a final like last year here? Is it a different match now?

MARAT SAFIN: I think so. Because I have more experience in the Finals. I played enough this year, and what, is nothing else. It's just experience. Probably I'm not gonna be so afraid of being in a final and not making a really good score on the court. I just gonna be there on the court. Not to say how many am I gonna win tomorrow. I'm gonna be there to win the tournament.I want it. I want to win one more tournament, especially here in Paris where I start my tennis career, at Roland Garros. It will be great, you know, to win here at least one tournament.

Q. It's the end of the season. Everybody's tired. Yet you seem to be playing your best tennis, and you look fresh. Are you as fresh as you look?

MARAT SAFIN: Because I start very late. (Laughter.)

Q. You played more than the others in the last six months.

MARAT SAFIN: Yes, but I played -- I didn't play first four months. I couldn't win one set, one game. So now I can, you know, finally I found confidence. I can play my best tennis. At least end of the year, why not?

Q. So you feel as fresh as you look?

MARAT SAFIN: No. (Laughter.)

Q. Would you have preferred in the term of the race, would you have preferred to play Guga in the final? Just for the race?

MARAT SAFIN: If, if, if, if, if. If it gonna rain tomorrow, everybody gonna die. I don't know. The fire gonna come. I think the winner is in the final. Who is the better player is in the final. So this case it was Mark. What can you do? You can't think about "If" Guga would win. It would be easier to play against him. I don't know. I have Mark, he won the match against Guga, he's playing better for Guga for today at least. So probably for you, for the people, it would be nice to see the final Kuerten and Safin. Who gonna have enough balls to win, "New Balls," to win the title? (Laughter.) But in this case, it was Mark and not the "New Balls." So that's it.
