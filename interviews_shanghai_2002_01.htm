2002 Tennis Masters Cup, Shanghai, China
November 12, 2002
Marat Safin - Carlos Moya (ESP) 4-6, 5-7
An interview with: MARAT SAFIN


QUESTION: Before the match started, you took about five to six minutes before starting the match. The atmosphere on court, does it affect your preparation for the match?

MARAT SAFIN: No, just they called us whenever, just we were waiting for the TV and whenever TV was ready, so went out on the court and that's why it took so long. So is not our fault.

QUESTION: After losing to Moya and next match you're going to meet Hewitt and Kim Clijsters won the WTA event by beating Serena Williams 2-0 today. Do you think that that will give Hewitt more motivation and how are you going to handle the next match?

MARAT SAFIN: First of all, I think that I'm playing the loser of that match, Costa-Hewitt, and if I play against Hewitt, which is going to be anyway because we're in the same group, I will still be playing against Hewitt, not against Kim Clijsters and Hewitt.

QUESTION: Your season obviously is going to last a bit longer than most people this year with the Davis Cup. How did you feel your form was today and are you happy with the way things are looking?

MARAT SAFIN: Well, for me, it's a very long season. I'm here in Shanghai and I still have three weeks to finish my year and actually is really, really long and I'm getting tired and I have no time for vacations because whenever I finish the Davis Cup, I have just 10 days of vacations and then I have to prepare myself for the next season and for me is not enough. So definitely take some weeks off during next year because otherwise -- when is too much tennis? You're getting tired and you cannot play at the same level during all the season, which is -- I think I'll have to think about my schedule for the next year and it's tiring, it's tiring because every time you have long matches, you have -- you win, you lose, but still when you lose, you're going a little bit down and then to find the motivation to come back is really difficult, especially when you have a lot of matches, which I have lately, I have been playing for so many weeks in a row and it's a tough schedule, really tough.

QUESTION: You broke Moya's serve quite easily and after that, you should have confidence to hold your serve, but what's the reason that you got broken back quite easily after that?

MARAT SAFIN: That's tennis. That's just -- it happens. It happens all the time when you're playing against big players, against players like Moya, and really felt like I had the match in my hands when I broke him back in the first set. I thought that's going to be it, have a big chance to come back and to win the first one and then let's see what happened in the second one, in the second set, but then unfortunately I didn't -- I had so many opportunities in the first set, I had so many opportunities in the second one and just I couldn't make them. So that's why I didn't deserve to win and unfortunately that's life.

QUESTION: You talked about having just 10 days after before you start your Australian campaign. Have you given any thought to the lead-in before the Australian Open, what your schedule will hold?

MARAT SAFIN: I didn't get anything. Sorry, can you repeat that again?

QUESTION: Do you know what lead-in events you will be playing before the Australian Open?

MARAT SAFIN: What event. Sydney. I'm playing Sydney.

QUESTION: Just one match?

MARAT SAFIN: Hopefully not one match. Whole tournament, you know. I hope to play a few matches and get prepared. I think it's the best. My schedule for next year, I decided to play Sydney. I think it's the right tournament to prepare myself for the Australian Open and I think it's nice city also, so I'll have great time there.

QUESTION: What was the difference between your semifinal win over Moya in Paris and losing to him today? What was the difference in the matches?

MARAT SAFIN: I don't think it's not much difference. There was also two three balls difference and here was the same thing because if I could have realised my opportunities in the first set and the second one, I should have won also in two, but unfortunately he has more opportunities in here. He was more lucky or just more, you know, like he gets his opportunities and he went for it and I have been waiting and I made a mistake. That's just two points that makes a difference. The score is 7-5, 6-4, could go the other way around, but today it went for his side, so I didn't think that I played really bad today. I had more opportunities, but wasn't -- the luck wasn't on my side. Let's put it this way.

QUESTION: What are your plans after Davis Cup? That's the first question. Second question is: when are you going to look for a girlfriend?

MARAT SAFIN: First question. I think I will stay at home because I'm tired of travelling. I have been travelling during all the year and for us to stay some days at home, it's more than vacations. Second question. I'm too busy with tennis. I have no time to have a girlfriend. I wish I have enough time to find one and will be great, but business is first for the moment.
